# No action that increases risk to uninvolved third parties

**Statement:**  
Operators must not take actions that increase risk to individuals or groups who are not part of the operation.

**Rationale:**  
- Ethical boundaries are part of system integrity.  
- Risk externalization creates long-term instability and reputational harm.

**Implications:**  
- Evaluate downstream effects before executing actions.  
- Reject shortcuts that shift risk onto others.

**Examples:**  
- Accept: Delaying an operation until safety is confirmed.  
- Reject: Proceeding because “it probably won’t matter.”
