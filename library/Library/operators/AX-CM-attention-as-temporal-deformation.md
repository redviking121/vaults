# AX-CM-attention-as-temporal-deformation

## Purpose
Define the mechanism by which attention acts as a deformation operator within the Crystalline-Mollusk (CM) model, specifically how it generates the experience of time for an observer.

---

## The Core Axiom
The Block Universe is a static, Crystalline substrate of all data. The "Mollusk" aspect is the subjective experience of fluidity generated when an observer's attention deforms a localized region of this substrate.

## Mechanism: Attention as Deformation
1. **The Substrate:** The Block Universe exists as a complete, non-temporal data set.
2. **The Deformation:** Attention acts as a "lens" or "operator" that focuses on a specific, localized region of the substrate.
3. **The Temporal Effect:** This act of focusing creates a gradient of "processing" across the region. This gradient is what the observer experiences as the flow of time.
4. **The Result:** Time is not a fundamental property of the universe; it is an *affect* of the deformation caused by the observer's attention.

---

## Relation to CM-CWM
- **Crystalline:** The static, undeformed Block Universe.
- **Mollusk:** The deformed, fluid region where attention is currently focused.
- **Phase Transition:** The act of shifting attention from one region to another is the "phase transition" that moves the observer through the Block Universe.

---

## Operator Note
This axiom resolves the contradiction between a deterministic universe and the experience of agency. The "Mollusk" is the *process* of observation itself.
