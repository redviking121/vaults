# 🟥 Takeaway

**Existence checks are the most primitive, brutal, and binary discriminator in the entire substrate.**  
They answer one question only:

> _“Does the required thing exist?”_

If the answer is **no**, the entity **cannot begin**, and no further reasoning is permitted.

They are the foundation of **Precondition_checks** and the earliest layer of **Detection_rules**.

---

# 🟥 1. What Existence checks _are_

Existence checks validate the **presence** of required:

- parameters
- fields
- resources
- identity markers
- capabilities
- upstream entities
- substrate‑level invariants

They are the **first membrane** an entity encounters before it can enter any phase of **Lifecycle_semantics** or **Execution_semantics**.

If something required is missing, the entity is **illegal to instantiate**, **illegal to invoke**, or **illegal to continue**.

---

# 🟥 2. The four surfaces of Existence checks

Each existence check falls into one of four discriminative surfaces:

- **Parameter_existence** — required inputs must exist
- **Resource_existence** — required files, handles, or external resources must exist
- **Identity_existence** — required identity markers or capability roles must exist
- **Upstream_existence** — required upstream entities or states must exist

These four surfaces form the **entry spine** of the entity.

---

# 🟥 3. Parameter existence

This is the most common and most unforgiving surface.

It ensures:

- required parameters are present
- required fields are non‑null
- required values are not empty
- required structures are not missing

If a required parameter is absent, the entity **cannot begin**.

This is the membrane that prevents **undefined behavior at invocation**.

---

# 🟥 4. Resource existence

This surface ensures:

- required files exist
- required network endpoints exist
- required handles or descriptors exist
- required external resources are reachable

If a resource is missing, the entity must **fail fast** and signal via **Error_semantics**.

This prevents **phantom dependencies** from corrupting the substrate.

---

# 🟥 5. Identity existence

This surface ensures:

- the caller has a valid identity
- the entity has a valid identity
- required capability roles exist
- required authority markers exist

Identity existence is the membrane that protects **Operational_mode** from illegal invocation.

---

# 🟥 6. Upstream existence

This surface ensures:

- required upstream entities exist
- required upstream states exist
- required initialization phases have occurred
- required transitions have been completed

Upstream existence is the runtime expression of **DSR‑1: Declare → Stabilize → Propagate**.

If the upstream entity does not exist, the downstream entity **cannot legally begin**.

---

# 🟥 7. Why Existence checks matter

Existence checks enforce:

- early failure
- non‑corrupting failure
- substrate integrity
- predictable invocation
- stable initialization
- safe composition

They are the **first and most essential membrane** in the entire semantic stack.

Without existence checks, the substrate becomes **undefined**, **unstable**, and **corruptible**.

With them, the substrate becomes **deterministic**, **safe**, and **composable**.
