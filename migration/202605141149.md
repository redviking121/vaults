# 🟥 1. What Field_presence _is_

Field_presence is a **binary discriminator** applied to a single field name within a structure.

It evaluates:

- whether the field name appears
- whether the field is included in the container
- whether the structure exposes that surface

It does **not** evaluate:

- nullness
- emptiness
- type correctness
- semantic validity
- shape

It is the purest form of **Existence_checks** at the field level.

# 🟥 3. How Field_presence is evaluated

The membrane performs a simple structural sweep:

1. Inspect the container

3. Emit a binary result

- **1** → field exists
- **0** → field absent

This is the same primitive used in **Field_presence_vector**, but applied to a single field rather than the entire schema family.

# 🟥 4. Field_presence vs. Parameter_presence vs. Structural_presence

These three surfaces form a hierarchy:

- **Parameter_presence** — does the parameter appear in the invocation
- **Structural_presence** — does the required container/path exist

Field_presence is the **lowest‑level** discriminator — the atomic unit of structural existence.
