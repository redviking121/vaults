# Engine Down Protocol ◉

When the main vault (Vault A) is unavailable, unstable, migrating, or undergoing refactor, switch to this protocol. The goal is continuity of command without multiplying mysteries or creating drift.

---

## 1. Switch Surfaces
Move operations to the Ops Vault immediately.
- Use this vault as the cockpit.
- Avoid opening or modifying Vault A until stable.
- Keep all captures and tasks here.

---

## 2. Capture Here, Not There
All new thoughts, tasks, and observations go into:
- [[quick-capture]]
- or new cards in [[../10_Cards/active/]]

Do not attempt to update or repair Vault A during instability.

---

## 3. Maintain Operational Awareness
Use the dashboards:
- [[cockpit]]
- [[today]]

These provide continuity without relying on Vault A’s structure.

---

## 4. Avoid Deep Edits
During engine-down conditions:
- Do not restructure projects.
- Do not modify lineage-heavy notes.
- Do not attempt plugin changes in Vault A.
- Do not move or rename folders in Vault A.

Ops Vault is for navigation, not surgery.

---

## 5. Track What Must Return to Vault A
As you work, maintain a list of items that must be reintegrated once the engine is back online.

Use:
- [[project-entrypoints]]
- A section in the daily review on the cockpit or today page.

Keep this list shallow and intentional.

---

## 6. Reintegration Ritual (When the Engine Returns)
Once Vault A is stable:
- Migrate cards back into their canonical locations.
- Recreate or merge notes as needed.
- Close loops created during engine-down.
- Resume normal operations.

Ops Vault remains untouched and ready for the next storm.

---

## 7. Operator Stance
Remember:
- Do not multiply mysteries.
- Do not fork the ontology.
- Do not chase the tail.
- Maintain continuity, not complexity.

The Ops Vault is the pea coat — worn only when needed, always ready, never heavy.
