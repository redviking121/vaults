# Fallback Ops ◉

General operating procedure when Vault A is unstable, unavailable, or undergoing change.  
This is the standing order set — the logic of the pea coat.

---

## 1. Operate From the Ops Vault
- Use this vault as the cockpit.
- Keep all captures, tasks, and triage here.
- Avoid interacting with Vault A until stable.

---

## 2. Maintain Continuity
Use the dashboards:
- [[cockpit]]
- [[today]]
- [[week]]

These provide situational awareness without relying on Vault A.

---

## 3. Keep Ontology Unified
- Use the same card grammar as Vault A.
- Do not invent new categories or statuses.
- Maintain lineage without multiplying mysteries.

---

## 4. Capture → Triage → Hold
- Capture in Inbox or new cards.
- Triage into active / waiting / blocked.
- Hold everything here until Vault A returns.

---

## 5. Reintegration Discipline
When the engine is back online:
- Migrate cards back.
- Recreate notes as needed.
- Close loops.
- Resume normal operations.

Ops Vault remains clean and ready.

---

## 6. Operator Stance
- Calm over clever.
- Continuity over complexity.
- Lineage over novelty.
- Reversibility over speed.

The Ops Vault is the fallback bridge — always ready, never heavy.
