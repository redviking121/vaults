# 🧭 Personnel Qualifications Onboarding Roadmap

**Takeaway:** A strong qualification program moves employees from _new hire → trained → assessed → certified → continuously competent_.

## 1. Pre‑Onboarding (Before Day 1)

This stage ensures the employee enters the organization with clarity and required baseline documentation.

- **Role definition & competency matrix** Identify required skills, certifications, safety requirements, and performance expectations.
- **Background checks & credential verification** Validate degrees, licenses, prior certifications, and work history.
    
- **Access provisioning** Accounts, equipment, security badges, system permissions.
    
- **Pre‑reading & orientation materials** Policies, SOPs, safety manuals, org chart, role overview.

## 2. Orientation & Compliance Foundations (Week 1)

Builds organizational alignment and ensures legal/regulatory compliance.

- **Company orientation** Mission, values, culture, policies, code of conduct.
    
- **Mandatory compliance training** Examples: safety, cybersecurity, harassment prevention, data privacy.
    
- **Introduction to SOPs & quality systems** How work is documented, audited, and controlled.
    
- **Initial safety qualification** PPE training, emergency procedures, hazard communication.
## 3. Role‑Specific Training (Weeks 1–4)

This is where the employee learns the actual job tasks.

### Core components:

- **Task‑based training modules** Each tied to a competency requirement.
    
- **Shadowing & supervised practice** Observe → assist → perform with supervision.
    
- **Systems & tools training** Software, machinery, workflows, documentation systems.
    
- **Knowledge checks** Short quizzes or practical demonstrations.

## 4. Competency Assessment & Qualification (Weeks 2–8)

This stage determines whether the employee can perform independently.

### Assessment types:

- **Written assessments** Policies, procedures, safety rules.
    
- **Practical demonstrations** Perform tasks to standard under observation.
    
- **Scenario‑based evaluations** Problem‑solving, troubleshooting, decision‑making.
    
- **Quality & accuracy checks** Output meets required standards.

### Qualification outcome:

- **Qualified** — can perform independently
    
- **Partially qualified** — needs targeted coaching
    
- **Not yet qualified** — retraining required

## 5. Certification & Authorization (Weeks 4–12)


For roles requiring formal authorization (e.g., equipment operation, regulated tasks).

- **Internal certification** Company‑issued qualification for specific tasks.
    
- **External certification** (if applicable) OSHA, HIPAA, ISO, industry‑specific credentials.
    
- **Authorization to perform critical tasks** Supervisor or manager signs off.
## 6. Integration Into Team & Performance Ramp‑Up (Months 1–6)

Ensures the employee becomes productive and aligned with team goals.

- **Mentorship assignment** A senior employee supports the transition.
    
- **Progressive workload increase** Start with low‑risk tasks → full responsibility.
    
- **Regular check‑ins** Weekly → biweekly → monthly.
    
- **Early performance goals** 30‑60‑90 day objectives.
## 7. Continuous Competency Maintenance (Ongoing)

Qualification isn’t one‑and‑done — it must be maintained.

- **Annual recertifications** Safety, compliance, equipment operation.
    
- **Refresher training** When SOPs or systems change.
    
- **Quality audits** Random or scheduled performance checks.
    
- **Skill development plans** Upskilling, cross‑training, leadership development.

# 📌 Optional Add‑Ons (Depending on Industry)
