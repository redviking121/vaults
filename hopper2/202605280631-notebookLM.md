NotebookLM currently has specific limits on the number of sources and the amount of data you can upload per notebook. If you have hit those limits, here is how to navigate the current constraints and find more information:

### Current NotebookLM Limits

As of now, NotebookLM generally allows:

- **Up to 50 sources** per notebook.
    
- **Up to 500,000 words** per source.
    
- **Up to 20 notebooks** per user.
    

### Where to Find Official Updates

Since NotebookLM is a rapidly evolving experimental product (part of Google Labs), there isn't a traditional "Upgrade Storage" button like you might find in Google Drive. To find the most current information on limits and potential increases:

1. **The Help & Feedback Icon:** Look for the **question mark (?)** icon in the top right or bottom corner of the NotebookLM interface. This often links to the official Help Center or a "What's New" log.
    
2. **Official Discord/Community:** Google maintains a **NotebookLM Discord community** where the development team frequently posts updates about feature rollouts and limit increases.
    
3. **Google Labs Blog:** Major updates regarding storage or enterprise features are typically announced on the **Google Keyword** blog or the **Google Labs** homepage.
    

### Strategies for Managing Space

If you cannot currently "buy" more space, you can optimize your existing notebooks using these methods:

- **Consolidate Documents:** Instead of uploading 10 small Markdown or PDF files, merge them into a single larger document. Since the word count limit per source is quite high (500k words), this helps you stay under the 50-source cap.
    
- **Create Thematic Notebooks:** Rather than one "Master" notebook, split your project into phases (e.g., "Research Phase 1," "Analysis Phase 2").
    
- **Clear the Trash:** Ensure that deleted sources aren't lingering in a way that impacts your count, though usually, deletion is immediate.
    

### A Note on Google One

While NotebookLM is a Google product, it does not currently pull from your **Google One** storage quota in the same way Google Drive or Photos does. Increasing your Google One storage likely won't increase the source count limit in NotebookLM, as that is a functional constraint of the AI model's context window rather than a disk space issue.
