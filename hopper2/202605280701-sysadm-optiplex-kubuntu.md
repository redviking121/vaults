That changes the game completely! Having a full-size tower chassis means we can throw out the restrictive "Low Profile" rule book. You have a massive physical advantage here.

Even though the motherboard and its proprietary power delivery are exactly as described—meaning you are still strictly bound by the **75-watt motherboard slot power limit** (no external 6-pin or 8-pin power cables)—you can now install a standard, **Full-Height (FH)** graphics card.

Full-height cards are much easier to find, generally cheaper, run cooler because they have room for larger cooling fans, and fit perfectly into your standard rear expansion slots.

### The New Best-Match Options

Since we are hunting for full-height, standard desktop cards that **do not require extra power connectors**, here are the top contenders that will drop right into your chassis and perfectly complement that 32 GB of RAM.

#### 1. The Open-Source Linux Standard: AMD Radeon RX 6400 (Standard Profile)

- **The Fit:** Now that you aren't restricted to tiny low-profile cards, you can pick up a standard single-slot or dual-slot version of this card.
    
- **The Match:** It still draws only ~53 watts entirely from the motherboard slot. Because the AMD drivers are built natively into the Linux kernel, it will work flawlessly out of the box with your current X11 setup and will handle the transition to Wayland in August without you having to manage any third-party driver installations.
    
- **Example Models:** _PowerColor Radeon RX 6400 ITX_ or standard _XFX/Sapphire_ variants.
    

#### 2. The Powerhouse Choice: NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3050 6GB (Standard Profile)

- **Crucial Warning:** Make sure it is explicitly the **6GB version**, not the older 8GB version. The 8GB version requires an external power cable. The newer **RTX 3050 6GB** was redesigned by NVIDIA specifically to run on a meager 70 watts of slot power alone.
    
- **The Performance:** This is the most powerful graphics processing architecture you can put in that machine without upgrading the power supply. With a full-size case, you can get a dual-fan version that runs whisper-quiet. It handles hardware decoding for modern web video codecs with absolute ease.
    
- **Example Models:** _ASUS Dual GeForce RTX 3050 6GB_ or _MSI GeForce RTX 3050 Ventus 2X 6G_.
    

#### 3. The Balanced Value: NVIDIA GTX 1650 (Standard Profile)

- **The Match:** A classic, full-height, slot-powered card that draws around 75 watts. While it's an older generation compared to the RTX 3050, standard-height GTX 1650 cards are highly abundant, very reliable under the proprietary NVIDIA Linux driver, and will effortlessly handle browsers, desktop animations, and multiple displays.
    

### One Quick Hardware Verification Before Buying

Because Dell loved to layout their older OptiPlex motherboards with unique configurations, open the side panel of your case and look at the main blue PCIe x16 slot where the graphics card will plug in.

Make sure there isn't an unusually tall component—like a massive CPU heatsink shroud or a hard drive cage—sitting directly in a straight line behind or right below that slot. Standard full-height cards have wider circuit boards and longer coolers, so you just want to verify that you have a few inches of clear, unobstructed horizontal space extending away from the slot toward the front of the case.

### Final Recommendation

If your primary goal is a **worry-free Linux experience** where system upgrades are completely seamless, go with a standard-height **AMD Radeon RX 6400**.

If you want the absolute **most capable hardware accelerator** that physically drops into that slot without needing a new power supply, look for a standard-height **NVIDIA RTX 3050 6GB**. Either choice turns that machine into a certified tank!
