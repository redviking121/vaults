### 1. High-Priority Frontmatter (YAML)

For incoming medical notes, standardizing your metadata right at the intake script level will allow you to query and filter the data easily later. Consider adding these specific keys:

- `type:` (e.g., study, lab-result, treatment-option, symptom-log)
    
- `status:` (e.g., under-review, verified, archive)
    
- `category:` (e.g., oncology, urology, immunotherapy)
    
- `date-of-event:` (for tracking specific appointments or tests)
    

### 2. Suggested Core Sub-Veins

To keep your atomic notes from scattering, you might want to create distinct clusters under your main Prostate Cancer map:

- **The Clinical Baseline:** Dedicated to tracking your specific numbers and pathology. This is where you store atomic notes on PSA (Prostate-Specific Antigen) trends, Gleason scores (which grade cell abnormalities), and staging definitions.
    
- **Treatment Modalities:** A comparative space to analyze different paths. You can separate these into atomic notes for active surveillance, surgical options, radiation therapies, and systemic treatments (like hormone therapy or immunotherapy).
    
- **Oncology & Urology Directory:** Quick-reference atomic notes for specific specialists, clinic locations, second opinions, and diagnostic facilities.
    
- **Questions & Trackers:** A running list of highly specific questions to export into your mobile notes right before you walk into a doctor's office.
