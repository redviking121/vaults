Letting it marinate is usually the best approach with architecture. Docker introduces a bit of initial friction—learning the commands and configuring the files—but once that "aha!" moment hits and you realize your environment is completely locked down, reproducible, and separate from your host machine's clutter, it's hard to go back to raw local execution.

Whenever you're ready to dig back into it, we can look at mapping specific local directory structures into the container, passing secure keys securely through an `.env` file, or writing a quick bash script to automate the execution of your Python routines so you barely even have to think about Docker being there.

Take your time with it! What aspect of the setup feels like it will require the biggest mental shift from how you normally run things?
