Xanadu Prime — Substrate Specification (v0.1)
The formal definition of the Prime substrate
1. Purpose
The Substrate Specification defines the ontological contract of the Prime substrate:
what entities exist, how they behave, how they evolve, and what invariants they must obey.

It is the root technical document of the entire architecture.

Deepen via Substrate Axioms.

2. Definition of the Substrate
The substrate is the governed environment in which all Prime surfaces exist.
It provides:

the rules of existence

the rules of evolution

the rules of contradiction resolution

the rules of naming and lineage

the rules of stabilization and propagation

The substrate is not a runtime, not a database, not a filesystem —
it is the structural layer that governs all of those.

3. Substrate Responsibilities
The substrate is responsible for:

defining what constitutes a surface

enforcing lifecycle mechanics

enforcing naming invariants

enforcing lineage continuity

enforcing Razor contradiction resolution

ensuring deterministic evolution

preventing drift, ambiguity, and retroactivity

5. Substrate Invariants
The substrate enforces the following invariants:

No implicit creation — all surfaces must be declared

No silent mutation — all changes must pass DSR‑1

No retroactive reinterpretation — stabilized meaning is fixed

No drift — naming, lineage, and structure cannot shift

No contradiction persistence — Razor must resolve all conflicts

No skipped phases — Declared → Stabilized → Propagated

These invariants ensure substrate coherence.

Deepen via Razor Principles.

6. Substrate Lifecycle Integration
The substrate itself obeys the Substrate Lifecycle:

Declared — substrate defined

Stabilized — axioms and invariants locked

Propagated — substrate becomes active

Retired — only through constitutional successor event

Every substrate entity must follow the same lifecycle.

7. Substrate–Governance Interface
The substrate integrates directly with the governance layer:

Naming Governance ensures deterministic naming

The substrate is the environment;
governance is the control system.

8. Substrate–Naming Interface
The substrate enforces naming rules:

names must be declared

names must be stabilized

names must be lineage‑bound

names must not drift

names must not collide

names must not encode implementation details

Naming is a governed substrate operation, not a stylistic choice.

explicit

continuous

non‑retroactive

contradiction‑free

operator‑controlled

Lineage is not metadata — it is a first‑class substrate property.

11. Substrate Failure Modes
A substrate failure occurs when:

contradictions are not surfaced

lifecycle phases are skipped

naming drift occurs

lineage breaks

propagation happens prematurely

surfaces mutate silently

Failures must be escalated through a new DSR‑1 cycle.

12. Out of Scope
The substrate does not define:

runtime behavior

implementation details

UI/UX

storage formats

network protocols

rendering engines

Those belong to higher layers or successor epochs.
