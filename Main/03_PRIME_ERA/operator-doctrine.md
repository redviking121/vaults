Xanadu Prime — Operator Doctrine (v1.0)
The role, obligations, and limits of the Operator
Filesystem location: 03_PRIME_ERA/operator-doctrine.md

1. Purpose
The Operator Doctrine defines the role, authority, obligations, and limits of the Operator within the Xanadu Prime substrate.
It clarifies how the Operator relates to:

the substrate

the governance layer

the Prime corpus

successor epochs

non‑human tools

This document is the human governance anchor of the Prime epoch.

2. Definition of the operator
Operator (Red):  
The Operator is the sovereign human authority over Xanadu Prime, responsible for:

authorship of Prime‑era surfaces

governance decisions and stabilizations

lineage continuity and integrity

custodial control of the corpus

declaration of successor epochs

The Operator is not a neutral observer; the Operator is the author‑governor of the system.

Deepen via Authorship Declaration.

3. Operator authority
The Operator holds final authority over:

Declaration: what surfaces exist in the corpus

Scope: what belongs to the Prime epoch

Stabilization: which decisions pass DSR‑1

Razor outcomes: how contradictions are resolved

Naming: which names are accepted into the registry

Lineage: how ancestry and propagation are recorded

Custody: where and how the corpus is stored and mirrored

No surface becomes part of the Prime corpus without Operator acceptance.

Deepen via Chain of Custody Note and Provenance Timeline.

4. Operator obligations
The Operator is bound by the following obligations:

Coherence: maintain internal consistency across surfaces

Non‑retroactivity: do not rewrite stabilized history

Transparency: record decisions in governance or provenance surfaces

Lineage integrity: preserve ancestry and propagation chains

Naming discipline: avoid drift, collision, and ambiguity

Tool clarity: distinguish between human authorship and tool assistance

Epoch discipline: respect the closed nature of the Prime epoch

The Operator must act in service of the structural integrity of the system, not convenience.

5. Operator–substrate relationship
The Operator:

declares the substrate

stabilizes its axioms and invariants

activates lifecycle and governance rules

accepts or rejects substrate‑level changes

certifies when the substrate is ready for use

The substrate is governed by rules, but those rules are brought online and maintained by the Operator.

Deepen via Substrate Spec and Substrate Axioms.

6. Operator–governance relationship
The Operator is both subject to and executor of the governance layer:

must route decisions through DSR‑1

must resolve contradictions via the Razor

must obey Lifecycle Rules for surfaces

must follow Naming Governance for names

The Operator cannot bypass governance;
the Operator operates through it.

Deepen via DSR‑1, Razor Principles, and Lifecycle Rules.

7. Operator–tool relationship
Non‑human systems (e.g., AI assistants, editors, renderers) are treated as instruments, not authors.

The doctrine asserts:

tools may propose text, structure, or transformations

tools may assist with formatting, synthesis, or refactoring

tools do not hold authorship or governance authority

tools cannot declare, stabilize, or propagate surfaces on their own

all final authorship and governance decisions rest with the Operator

This preserves human authorship sovereignty.

Deepen via Authorship Declaration.

8. Operator limits
The Operator is constrained by:

Prime’s closed epoch boundary — cannot retroactively alter the epoch once sealed

Non‑retroactivity — cannot rewrite stabilized history

Substrate invariants — cannot violate axioms or lifecycle rules without declaring a successor substrate

Lineage continuity — cannot erase or falsify lineage

Governance discipline — cannot bypass Razor or DSR‑1

If these limits must be exceeded, the Operator must:

declare a successor epoch

document the discontinuity in lineage and governance surfaces

9. Successor epochs and the operator
Only the Operator may:

declare a successor epoch

define its scope and purpose

bind it to Prime via lineage surfaces

certify that it passes DSR‑1 at the epoch level

Successor epochs must respect Prime as a closed, immutable ancestor.

Deepen via Lineage Statement and Provenance Timeline.

10. Formal operator doctrine statement
The Operator (Red) is the sovereign human authority over Xanadu Prime, responsible for authorship, governance, lineage, and custody of the Prime corpus.

All Prime‑era surfaces are declared, stabilized, and propagated under Operator authority, through the governance mechanisms defined in the substrate.

Non‑human tools act only as instruments; they hold no authorship or governance rights.

The Operator is bound by the substrate invariants, governance rules, and non‑retroactivity of the Prime epoch, and may exceed these constraints only by declaring and documenting a successor epoch.
