Xanadu Prime — Substrate Axioms (Stub v0.1)
1. Purpose
Substrate Axioms define the non‑negotiable truths of the Prime substrate.
They are not derived, argued, or inferred — they are declared by the operator and form the ground upon which all governance, lifecycle, naming, and structural rules stand.

Axioms are the root of the ontology.

2. Nature of Axioms
A substrate axiom must be:

irreducible — cannot be simplified

universal — applies to all surfaces

stable — does not drift over time

operator‑declared — never emergent

If a statement can be debated, negotiated, or derived, it is not an axiom.

3. Core Axioms (Prime Epoch)
3.1 Axiom 1 — Structure Precedes Substance
The substrate defines shape first, content second.
No substance may exist without a declared structure.

Deepen via Repo Standards.

3.2 Axiom 2 — Nothing Exists Implicitly
All substrate elements must be explicitly declared.
No hidden state. No silent creation. No emergent behavior.

Deepen via DSR‑1.

3.3 Axiom 3 — Lineage Must Be Preserved
Every artifact has a lineage chain.
No change may break lineage without explicit dissolution.

Deepen via Lifecycle Rules.

3.4 Axiom 4 — Contradictions Must Be Resolved
Contradictions cannot persist in the substrate.
They must be surfaced and resolved through the Razor.

Deepen via Razor Principles.

3.5 Axiom 5 — Operator Primacy
The operator defines truth.
The substrate does not infer, override, or assume intent.

This is the sovereignty rule of the system.

4. Axiom Interactions
Axioms interact through:

structure (Axiom 1)

declaration (Axiom 2)

lineage (Axiom 3)

resolution (Axiom 4)

authority (Axiom 5)

These interactions form the substrate’s governance geometry.

Deepen via Lifecycle Semantics.

5. Axiom Violations
A violation occurs when:

content appears without structure

a change occurs without declaration

lineage is broken silently

contradictions persist

the system overrides operator intent

Violations must be escalated through DSR‑1 and Razor stabilization.

6. Axiom Extension Rules
New axioms may be introduced only when:

they are irreducible

they do not contradict existing axioms

they are required for substrate stability

they pass Razor stabilization

the operator explicitly declares them

Axioms are rare.
Extensions are exceptional.

7. Out of Scope
Axioms do not define:

workflows

doctrine

runtime behavior

scanner/vessel logic

implementation details

Axioms define truth, not behavior.
