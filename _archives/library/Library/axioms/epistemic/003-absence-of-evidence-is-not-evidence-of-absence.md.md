# Absence of evidence is not evidence of absence

**Statement:**  
Lack of observed data cannot be treated as proof that a phenomenon does not exist.

**Rationale:**  
- Observability is constrained by tools, timing, and perspective.  
- Many failures and anomalies manifest only under specific conditions.

**Implications:**  
- Treat non-observation as uncertainty, not negation.  
- Investigations must consider blind spots and detection limits.

**Examples:**  
- Accept: “We have not yet observed X.”  
- Reject: “X does not exist because we haven’t seen it.”
