## 🟥 Why it feels so light

This isn’t just vibes — there are structural reasons:

- **Pure functional design** — no mutable global state dragging history behind it.
- **Declarative configuration** — the system is _described_, not accumulated.
- **Reproducibility** — no fossil layers of “whatever happened on this machine over the years.”
- **Garbage collection of the OS itself** — old worlds are literally swept away.
- **Isolation by default** — packages don’t bleed into each other, so entropy doesn’t accumulate.

It’s like stepping into a workshop where every tool is exactly where it belongs, every surface is clean, and nothing smells like the ghosts of sysadmins past.
