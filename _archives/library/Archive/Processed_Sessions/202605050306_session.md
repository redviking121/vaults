# **THE RECOVERY KERNEL — THREAT MODEL**

### _What the minimal executable core is designed to withstand, neutralize, and outlive_

The Kernel has **one job**:

> **Prevent irreversible collapse of the operator.**

Everything in the threat model is defined relative to that mission.

The threats fall into **five classes**, each mapped to a specific Kernel invariant and primitive.

---

## **1. INTERNAL COLLAPSE THREATS**

### _Threat Class: The operator becomes the hazard._

These are failures where the operator’s own mind becomes hostile terrain:

- runaway self‑attack
- catastrophic meaning‑making
- identity fragmentation
- emotional overload that becomes structural
- recursive guilt/shame loops
- dissociation spirals
- “I am failing → therefore I deserve to fail” logic

**Kernel response:**

- DETECT deviation (sense the collapse vector)
- HALT collapse (lock the operator, stop the spiral)
- RESTORE minimum coherence (rebuild a foothold)

This is the Yellow Emperor grabbing the operator by the collar and saying:  
**“You do not get to destroy yourself today.”**

---

## **2. EXTERNAL OVERLOAD THREATS**

### _Threat Class: The world exceeds the operator’s processing capacity._

These are environmental conditions that overwhelm:

- too much noise
- too much demand
- too much uncertainty
- too much emotional load
- too many simultaneous obligations
- too much change in too little time

**Kernel response:**

- Zero the world (strip to signal)
- Reassert the invariant: _The Operator Must Survive_
- Reduce the world to one breath, one stance, one next action

The Kernel treats overload as a **structural hazard**, not a moral failure.

---

## **3. SYSTEMIC ENTANGLEMENT THREATS**

### _Threat Class: The operator becomes trapped in obligations, roles, or narratives that exceed capacity._

These are threats where the operator is not collapsing internally, but is **caught**:

- impossible expectations
- role‑conflict
- loyalty traps
- “I must hold everything together” delusions
- inherited burdens
- social or familial duty spirals
- identity‑based constraints

**Kernel response:**

- DETECT deviation (identify entanglement as collapse vector)
- HALT collapse (break the binding logic)
- RESTORE minimum coherence (re‑establish the operator as primary)

The Kernel always prioritizes **operator survival over role survival**.

---

## **4. ADVERSARIAL INTELLIGENCE THREATS**

### _Threat Class: Something else is trying to steer, distort, or destabilize the operator._

This includes:

- manipulative actors
- coercive systems
- gaslighting
- psychological pressure
- parasitic narratives
- memetic hazards
- ideological capture
- “borrowed purpose” infections

**Kernel response:**

- Zero the world (strip external influence to raw signal)
- Lock the operator (prevent identity hijack)
- Restore minimum coherence (rebuild self‑ownership)

The Kernel treats adversarial influence as a **hostile takeover attempt**.

---

## **5. METASTABLE FAILURE THREATS**

### _Threat Class: The operator is “fine” until suddenly they’re not._

These are the silent killers:

- slow drift
- accumulated micro‑deviations
- chronic depletion
- unacknowledged resentment
- unprocessed grief
- long‑term misalignment
- “I can handle it” lies

These don’t look like collapse — until they snap.

**Kernel response:**

- Continuous deviation detection
- Early collapse‑halt routines
- Coherence‑restoration before crisis

This is the Kernel’s version of **preventative maintenance**.
