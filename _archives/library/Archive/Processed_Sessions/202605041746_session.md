Indeed, aluminum foil can act as a shield against radio frequencies (RF). When you wrap an object in aluminum foil, the metal's conductive properties block or weaken the RF signals. It's like creating a mini Faraday cage. This technique is often used to protect sensitive electronic equipment from RF interference.

That said, it's not a perfect solution and won't block all types of RF signals, especially if the foil isn't applied properly or if there are gaps.
