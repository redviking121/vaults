# 🟥 **THE SCALABLE STORY PATTERN (Universal Template)**

_(Works for 30‑min radio, 60‑min TV, 120‑min film, novels, arcs, anything)_

## ⭐ **1. The Hook**

**Purpose:** Seize attention instantly.  
**Function:** Present a _problem_, a _mystery_, or a _moment of imbalance_.  
**Space:** 1–5% of total runtime.

**Template:**

- A character wants something.
- Something blocks them.
- The world tilts.

---

## ⭐ **2. The Frame**

**Purpose:** Establish the world, tone, and rules.  
**Function:** Give the audience the minimum context needed to follow the plot.  
**Space:** 5–15%.

**Template:**

- Who the protagonist is
- What “normal” looks like
- What’s at stake if normal breaks

---

## ⭐ **3. The Inciting Shift**

**Purpose:** Break the frame.  
**Function:** Introduce the event that forces the protagonist to act.  
**Space:** 10–20%.

**Template:**

- A disruption
- A call to action
- A refusal or hesitation
- A push that forces movement

---

## ⭐ **4. The Rising Knot**

**Purpose:** Complicate everything.  
**Function:** Escalate tension, deepen character, reveal hidden layers.  
**Space:** 30–40%.

**Template:**

- First attempt fails
- Second attempt reveals new information
- Third attempt changes the stakes
- Allies, enemies, and internal conflict emerge

---

## ⭐ **5. The Breaking Point**

**Purpose:** Corner the protagonist.  
**Function:** Force a choice that cannot be undone.  
**Space:** 5–10%.

**Template:**

- All paths collapse
- A truth is revealed
- The protagonist must transform or die (literally or metaphorically)

---

## ⭐ **6. The Turn**

**Purpose:** The decisive action.  
**Function:** The protagonist commits to the new self.  
**Space:** 10–15%.

**Template:**

- A bold move
- A confrontation
- A sacrifice or leap
- A moment of irreversible change

---

## ⭐ **7. The Resolution**

**Purpose:** Show the consequences.  
**Function:** Close the loop, open the next one.  
**Space:** 5–10%.

**Template:**

- The world stabilizes
- The character is changed
- A new question emerges (optional)

---

# 🟥 **THE META‑PATTERN (Why This Scales)**

## ⭐ **Compression**

Short form = fewer beats per section.  
Long form = more beats per section.  
The pattern stays the same.

## ⭐ **Elasticity**

Each section can expand or contract depending on medium.

## ⭐ **Character Priority**

Plot is just the pressure applied to reveal character.

## ⭐ **The Loop**

Every story ends by opening the next question.
