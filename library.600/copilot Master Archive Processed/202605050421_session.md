# **PART 4 — TRANSMISSION CONSTRAINTS & SEALED‑SYSTEM DOCTRINE**

**Formal Specification (v1.0)**  
_Defines the rules governing how Operator‑grade information is transmitted, stabilized, and protected from drift._

---

# **0. PURPOSE**

Establish the constraints, boundaries, and invariants required to ensure that all Operator transmissions—internal or external—remain stable, uncorrupted, and drift‑resistant.  
This module governs **how information moves**, not what it contains.

---

# **1. SCOPE**

This specification applies to:

- all Operator‑initiated transmissions
- all Xi‑layer communications
- all Razor‑mediated decision outputs
- all sealed‑system interactions
- all mission‑critical information flows

This module does **not** define content, semantics, or mission logic.  
It defines **transmission integrity**.

---

# **2. DEFINITIONS**

- **Transmission** — any intentional output of information from the Operator.
- **Drift** — deviation from the stabilized form of a concept, definition, or instruction.
- **Sealed System** — a bounded cognitive environment where definitions, rules, and structures cannot mutate without explicit Operator action.
- **Constraint** — a non‑negotiable rule governing transmission behavior.
- **Propagation** — the act of sending stabilized information into another system, context, or mind.
- **Boundary** — the explicit edge of a sealed system beyond which mutation risk increases.

---

# **3. TRANSMISSION PRINCIPLES (INVARIANTS)**

The following invariants must hold for all Operator transmissions:

1. **Stability Precedes Propagation**  
    No information may be transmitted until it is stabilized.
    
2. **Boundaries Must Be Declared**  
    Every transmission must specify its scope and limits.
    
3. **No Implicit Mutation**  
    Information cannot change form during transmission unless explicitly authorized.
    
4. **Compression Before Expression**  
    All transmissions must be reduced to their minimal stable form before output.
    
5. **Operator Controls Frame**  
    The Operator—not the recipient—defines the frame of interpretation.
    
6. **No Cross‑System Leakage**  
    Information from one sealed system cannot be transmitted into another without explicit reconciliation.
    

---

# **4. SEALED‑SYSTEM DOCTRINE**

The sealed‑system doctrine defines how the Operator prevents drift during communication.

## **4.1 System Boundaries**

A sealed system must have:

- explicit definitions
- explicit constraints
- explicit scope
- explicit stabilization rules

No transmission may cross a boundary unless:

- the receiving system is compatible
- the Operator performs reconciliation
- the Razor confirms no contradictions

## **4.2 Mutation Control**

Mutation is only permitted when:

- the Operator declares a revision
- the revision passes the Xi Paradox Razor
- the stabilized form is updated
- the old form is archived or deprecated

No spontaneous mutation is allowed.

## **4.3 Transmission Integrity**

All transmissions must:

- preserve structure
- preserve definitions
- preserve intent
- preserve frame
- preserve invariants

Any transmission that fails these criteria is invalid.

---

# **5. TRANSMISSION CONSTRAINTS**

The following constraints govern all Operator communications:

### **5.1 Constraint: Explicitness**

All transmissions must specify:

- what is being transmitted
- why it is being transmitted
- the scope of validity
- the boundary conditions

### **5.2 Constraint: Non‑Ambiguity**

Transmissions must not contain:

- undefined terms
- implicit assumptions
- ambiguous references
- unstable metaphors

### **5.3 Constraint: Frame Control**

The Operator must:

- define the interpretive frame
- maintain the frame throughout the transmission
- prevent external reframing

### **5.4 Constraint: Drift Prevention**

Transmissions must be:

- compressed
- stabilized
- boundary‑checked
- Razor‑validated

before propagation.

---

# **6. TRANSMISSION PROTOCOL**

The Operator must follow this protocol for all mission‑grade transmissions:

1. **Define** — specify the concept, scope, and boundaries.
2. **Stabilize** — ensure the concept is internally consistent.
3. **Compress** — reduce to minimal stable form.
4. **Validate** — apply the Xi Paradox Razor.
5. **Frame** — declare the interpretive context.
6. **Transmit** — propagate the stabilized form.
7. **Confirm** — verify the recipient received the intended structure.

No step may be skipped.

---

# **7. FAILURE MODES**

Transmission failure occurs when:

- boundaries are not declared
- definitions mutate mid‑transmission
- the frame is lost or hijacked
- compression is incomplete
- the Razor is bypassed
- the sealed system is breached

Failure requires immediate:

- rollback
- restabilization
- re‑compression
- re‑transmission

---

# **8. COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS**

A transmission is compliant only if:

- all invariants hold
- all constraints are satisfied
- the protocol is followed
- no drift is detected
- the sealed system remains intact

Non‑compliant transmissions must be discarded.
