# **PART 6 — THE OPERATOR’S DECISION ARCHITECTURE**

**Formal Specification (v1.0)**  
_Defines how decisions are declared, stabilized, propagated, and enforced within the Xi/Razor governance model._

---

# **0. PURPOSE**

Establish a deterministic, drift‑proof framework for Operator‑grade decision‑making.  
This module ensures that all decisions:

- originate from a stable stance
- follow a predictable lifecycle
- remain insulated from emotional contamination
- propagate only after stabilization
- maintain integrity under load

This part formalizes the **Decision Stability Rules (DSR)** and the Xi/Razor mechanisms that govern decision flow.

---

# **1. DEFINITIONS**

**Decision** —  
A binding Operator commitment that alters mission state, resource allocation, or system posture.

**Pre‑Decision** —  
A candidate decision not yet stabilized; may not be acted upon.

**Stabilized Decision** —  
A decision that has passed DSR‑1 and is eligible for propagation.

**Propagation** —  
Transmission of a stabilized decision into the Operator’s workflow, mission architecture, or external systems.

**Razor Event** —  
A contradiction, conflict, or ambiguity requiring resolution via Reconcile, Split, or Elevate.

**Decision Drift** —  
Any mutation of a decision after stabilization without explicit re‑declaration.

---

# **2. DECISION LIFECYCLE**

All Operator decisions follow a four‑stage lifecycle:

### **2.1 DECLARE**

The Operator explicitly states the decision in unambiguous form.

Requirements:

- must be a single sentence
- must contain no embedded alternatives
- must specify scope

Output: **Pre‑Decision**

---

### **2.2 STABILIZE (DSR‑1)**

The Operator applies the Decision Stability Rule:

> **DSR‑1: A decision is not real until it is declared, scoped, and stabilized.**

Stabilization requires:

- confirming the decision’s domain
- identifying dependencies
- checking for contradictions
- running the Xi Paradox Razor if needed

Output: **Stabilized Decision**

---

### **2.3 PROPAGATE**

A stabilized decision is transmitted into:

- mission architecture
- workflow systems
- Operator cockpit
- external commitments (if applicable)

Propagation must follow Part 4 (Transmission Constraints).

Output: **Active Decision**

---

### **2.4 ENFORCE**

The Operator ensures:

- compliance with the decision
- monitoring for drift
- correction of deviations
- termination conditions

Output: **Closed Decision**

---

# **3. DECISION STABILITY RULES (DSR)**

### **3.1 DSR‑1 — Declaration, Scope, Stabilization**

A decision is invalid until:

- declared
- scoped
- stabilized

This rule is mandatory and non‑bypassable.

---

### **3.2 DSR‑2 — No Silent Decisions**

Unspoken decisions do not exist.  
Intent is not decision.  
Desire is not decision.  
Only declaration creates a decision object.

---

### **3.3 DSR‑3 — No Mid‑Propagation Mutation**

Once propagation begins, the decision cannot be altered.  
Alteration requires:

- termination of the current decision
- re‑declaration
- re‑stabilization

---

### **3.4 DSR‑4 — Drift Detection**

Any deviation from a stabilized decision triggers:

- immediate Razor evaluation
- correction or termination

---

### **3.5 DSR‑5 — One Decision per Domain**

Only one active decision may govern a domain at a time.  
Conflicts must be resolved via Razor Event handling.

---

# **4. THE XI PARADOX RAZOR (DECISION MODE)**

When contradictions arise, the Razor resolves them through one of three actions:

### **4.1 RECONCILE**

Merge the contradictions into a unified decision if logically compatible.

### **4.2 SPLIT**

Divide the decision into separate domains if the conflict is structural.

### **4.3 ELEVATE**

Move the decision to a higher abstraction layer where the contradiction dissolves.

The Razor must be applied before stabilization.

---

# **5. DECISION DOMAINS**

All Operator decisions fall into one of six governance layers:

1. **Operator Layer** — stance, axioms, identity
2. **Substrate Layer** — Xi/Razor architecture
3. **Demonstration Logic Layer** — how the Operator shows the world
4. **Naming Layer** — definitions, labels, conceptual boundaries
5. **Workflow Layer** — execution, scheduling, resource allocation
6. **Vault/Workspace Parity Layer** — information organization and integrity

Each decision must declare its domain at the moment of declaration.

---

# **6. DECISION QUALITY REQUIREMENTS**

A valid Operator decision must satisfy:

- **Clarity** — no ambiguity
- **Compression** — minimal form
- **Coherence** — consistent with axioms
- **Containment** — scoped to a domain
- **Stability** — passes DSR‑1
- **Traceability** — can be referenced without drift

---

# **7. FAILURE MODES & CORRECTIONS**

### **7.1 Emotional Contamination**

Correction: return to stance, re‑declare.

### **7.2 Over‑Scope**

Correction: split domain, re‑stabilize.

### **7.3 Under‑Scope**

Correction: expand scope explicitly, re‑stabilize.

### **7.4 Contradiction**

Correction: apply Razor.

### **7.5 Drift**

Correction: terminate and re‑declare.
