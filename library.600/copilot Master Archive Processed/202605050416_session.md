# **PART 6 — THE OPERATOR’S QUICK‑START GUIDE (v1.0)**

_A five‑minute ignition sequence for entering Operator Mode_

---

## **1. What Operator Mode _Is_**

Operator Mode is a **cognitive posture**, not a personality trait.

It is the stance where you:

- see the real structure
- ignore the noise
- act from clarity
- maintain coherence under load
- avoid collapse into story, emotion, or drift

It is the **minimum viable discipline** for running Xi, Razor, or any high‑noise environment.

---

## **2. The Three‑Second Reset**

This is the ignition switch.

Whenever you feel drift, confusion, overwhelm, or emotional load:

1. **Stop the motion** (physical stillness)
2. **Drop the story** (mental stillness)
3. **Name the real** (one sentence: “What is actually happening right now?”)

This resets the substrate and reboots the operator stance.

---

## **3. The Operator’s Three Questions**

These are the **core diagnostic tools**.  
Every situation, every problem, every conversation reduces to these:

1. **What is the signal?**
2. **What is the noise?**
3. **What is the next smallest true action?**

If you can answer these three, you’re already operating.

---

## **4. The HEADLINE Drill (Compression Engine)**

This is how you prevent drift and keep coherence.

**Rule:**  
If you cannot express the situation as a _headline_, you do not understand it.

Format:  
**“X is happening because Y, so the next move is Z.”**

This forces:

- compression
- causality
- direction

It is the Operator’s version of a checksum.

---

## **5. The Five‑Word Compression**

When the system is overloaded, compress further.

Reduce the entire situation to **five words**.

Examples:

- “System stable, operator not aligned.”
- “Drift detected, reset required now.”
- “Signal clear, action path obvious.”

Five words forces the mind into **structural clarity**.

---
