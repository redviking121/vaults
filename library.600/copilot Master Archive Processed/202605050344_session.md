# **2. File‑by‑File Breakdown**

## **SOUL.md**

The _essence_ file. Defines the student’s AI‑interpretable identity.

**Contains:**

- Name, role, age, developmental stage
- Communication style (kid‑friendly, metaphor preferences, pacing)
- Cognitive strengths & blind spots
- Motivational profile
- Boundaries (e.g., “avoid overwhelming with abstraction”)
- “Voice” of the student’s AI twin (if used)

This is the **anchor**.  
Change SOUL.md → the entire jacket shifts.

---

## **CHARACTER.json**

The structured, machine‑readable version.

**Contains:**

- Biographical data
- Behavioral traits
- Learning style
- Plugin/extension hooks
- Flags for special needs or accommodations
- Tags for curriculum routing (e.g., “visual thinker”, “pattern‑first”, “slow‑start fast‑finish”)

This is the **schema** that other systems can ingest.

---

## **SYSTEM_PROMPT.md**

Defines how the AI should interact _when acting as the student’s companion or tutor_.

**Contains:**

- Instructional tone
- Allowed metaphors
- How to scaffold difficulty
- How to respond to confusion
- How to encourage without patronizing
- How to maintain consistency across sessions

This is the **behavioral contract**.

---

## **AI.ai**

The portable personality export.

**Contains:**

- Memory settings
- Persona parameters
- Safety rails
- Conversational defaults
- Long‑term preferences
- Any custom embeddings or vectors (if supported)

This is the **loadable personality capsule**.

---

## **PERSONALITY.yaml**

Used by local AI frameworks.

**Contains:**

- Traits
- Temperament
- Cognitive mode preferences
- Interaction patterns
- “If X, respond with Y” rules
- Optional: archetype alignment (Explorer, Harmonizer, Analyst, etc.)

This is the **config layer**.

---
