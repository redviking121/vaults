# 🟥 Takeaway

**Presence checks answer the question: “Is the thing _present_ in the invocation surface?”**

# 🟥 1. What Presence checks _are_

Presence checks validate that a required field, parameter, or structure **appears at all** in the invocation surface.

They do not evaluate:

- correctness
- validity
- type
- shape
- content
- semantics

They only evaluate **presence**.

Presence checks are the membrane that prevents the entity from entering **undefined invocation** due to missing surfaces.

---

# 🟥 2. The three surfaces of Presence checks

Each presence check falls into one of three discriminative surfaces:

- **Field_presence** — does the field exist
- **Parameter_presence** — does the parameter appear in the call
- **Structural_presence** — does the required structure exist at the expected path

These are the earliest discriminators in the invocation membrane.

---

# 🟥 3. Field presence

Field presence ensures:

- the field name exists
- the field is included in the structure
- the field is not omitted

It does **not** check whether the field is null, empty, or malformed — only that it exists.

This is the membrane that prevents **missing‑field invocation**.

---

# 🟥 4. Parameter presence

Parameter presence ensures:

- the parameter appears in the invocation
- the caller provided it
- the entity can reference it

If the parameter is absent, the entity is **illegal to invoke**.

This is the earliest discriminator in **Invocation_rules**.

---

# 🟥 5. Structural presence

Structural presence ensures:

- the required structure exists
- the required path exists
- the required container exists

It does not validate the contents — only the **presence of the container**.

This prevents **phantom‑structure invocation**, where the entity attempts to operate on a structure that does not exist.

---

# 🟥 6. Why Presence checks matter

Presence checks enforce:

- early detection of missing surfaces
- prevention of undefined invocation
- stable entry into deeper membranes
- clean separation between “missing” and “invalid”
- deterministic invocation behavior

Presence checks are the **first membrane** in the invocation chain — the point where the entity either _may proceed_ or is _illegal to begin_.
