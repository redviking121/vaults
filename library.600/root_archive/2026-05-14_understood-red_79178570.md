## 🧹 **Operator‑Grade Housekeeping Pass (Library Edition)**

Here’s the minimal, high‑signal sequence that keeps you from making things worse while you fix the structure.

### **1. Identify the “stray cluster”**

You just dropped files into the wrong structure.  
Before touching anything:

- note _which_ folder they landed in
- note _what species_ they are (journal, notes, ops docs, mythos, etc.)
- confirm whether filenames still carry their timestamps or identifiers

This prevents accidental double‑moves.

---

### **2. Freeze the rest of the vault**

Don’t reorganize anything else.  
Don’t rename folders.  
Don’t fix metadata.

Just isolate the mistake.

This keeps the librarian from chasing ghosts.

---

### **3. Move the strays into a **single quarantine folder**

Create (or reuse):

`_incoming/`  
or  
`_quarantine/`

Drop all misplaced files there.  
This gives you a clean staging area and restores the vault’s top‑level clarity instantly.

---

### **4. Re‑sort from quarantine into the correct structure**

Now that the mess is contained:

- journals → `library/`
- ops docs → `Ops‑Vault/`
- mythos → `Myth Ledger/`
- anything else → appropriate namespace

This is the safe moment to re‑file.

---

### **5. Verify the indexer (chronoindex.py) won’t choke**

Since your indexer runs via cron, make sure:

- filenames still follow the timestamp pattern
- no duplicates were created
- no partial files exist

If everything looks clean, the next cron run will self‑heal the index.
