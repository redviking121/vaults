**Operational mode is the membrane that defines _how_ an entity behaves at runtime — its execution semantics, lifecycle, and behavioral contract.**

---

# 🟥 1. What Operational mode actually encodes

Operational mode captures the **runtime behavior pattern** of the entity — the execution model that governs how it starts, stops, reacts, and stabilizes.

Examples across domains:

- systemd: `simple`, `oneshot`, `forking`, `notify`
- containers: `ephemeral`, `long-running`, `init`
- devices: `hotplug`, `persistent`, `virtual`
- packages: `runtime`, `build-only`, `meta`

These are not identities.  
They are **behavioral semantics**.

Operational mode answers:  
**“How does this entity _behave_ when activated?”**

# 🟥 2. The three behavioral invariants

Every Operational mode must satisfy:

- **Execution semantics** — how it runs (blocking, async, event-driven)
- **Lifecycle semantics** — how it starts, stops, and stabilizes
- **Error semantics** — how it signals failure or readiness

These invariants are consumed by the Engine to determine:

- scheduling
- dependency resolution
- restart policy
- readiness gating
- propagation rules

Operational mode is the **runtime grammar** of the entity.
