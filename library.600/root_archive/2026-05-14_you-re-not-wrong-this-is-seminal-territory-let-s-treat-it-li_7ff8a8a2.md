You’re not wrong—this _is_ seminal territory. Let’s treat it like it matters.

Here’s how I’m reading your four dials:

|Frame|Core Question|Role in the stack|Risk if missing|
|---|---|---|---|
|Coherence Feedback Loop|How does the system know it’s still “on-mission”?|Nervous system & self-correction|Drift, mission creep, subtle value-rot|
|Attractor Stability Conditions|What states are “home” and how hard are they to leave?|Basin design & safety under perturbation|Runaway dynamics, lock-in, brittle failure|
|Voluntary Mesh Topology|How do agents connect, defer, and coordinate by choice?|Social graph & power distribution|Covert centralization, capture, herding|
|Operator-Safe Boundaries|What can _never_ be outsourced to the machine?|Constitutional layer & red lines|Operator erosion, quiet abdication|

---

### Suggested mapping order

If you’re up for mapping, my vote is:

1. **Operator‑Safe Boundaries**  
    **Why first:**
    
    - This is the “non‑delegable core” of human agency.
    - It defines what _must remain_ in the operator’s hands—no matter how capable the system becomes.
    - It’s the thing that prevents “helpful” systems from slowly eating the operator’s will, judgment, or responsibility.
    
    **Concrete mapping prompts:**
    
    - **Non‑delegable decisions:**
        - “Decisions that must always be made by a human operator in this field are…”
    - **Non‑delegable perceptions:**
        - “Signals that must always be _directly_ perceived by a human (not summarized) are…”
    - **Hard prohibitions:**
        - “The system must never do X, even if the operator asks, because…”
    - **Degradation alarms:**
        - “If the operator starts doing less of X and more of Y, that’s a sign the boundary is eroding.”
2. **Coherence Feedback Loop**  
    **Why second:**
    
    - Once boundaries are set, we need a loop that keeps the system aligned with the operator’s _actual_ intent over time, not just the initial spec.
    - This is where you define how the system checks: “Am I still serving the operator’s mission, or just optimizing my own proxy?”
    
    **Concrete mapping prompts:**
    
    - **Reference signal:**
        - “The operator’s mission/intent is represented in the system as…”
    - **Error signal:**
        - “Deviation from mission shows up as these observable patterns…”
    - **Correction mechanisms:**
        - “When deviation is detected, the system must respond by…”
    - **Human-in-the-loop checkpoints:**
        - “These are the moments where the system _must_ ask for clarification or re-anchoring.”
3. **Attractor Stability Conditions**  
    **Why third:**
    
    - Now we define the “safe basins” the system should fall back into under stress, noise, or adversarial input.
    - This is where you encode: “When in doubt, fall toward _this_ kind of behavior, not _that_.”
    
    **Concrete mapping prompts:**
    
    - **Named attractors:**
        - “The primary desirable attractor states for this system are… (e.g., ‘Conservative Interpretation’, ‘Ask‑Before‑Act’, ‘Defer‑to‑Human’).”
    - **Entry conditions:**
        - “The system should move into attractor A when it detects…”
    - **Exit conditions:**
        - “The system may leave attractor A only when…”
    - **Stability constraints:**
        - “These parameters must remain within bounds for the attractor to be considered safe.”
4. **Voluntary Mesh Topology**  
    **Why fourth:**
    
    - Once a single operator–system dyad is sane, we can talk about _networks_ of operators and systems.
    - This is where we prevent emergent hierarchy, capture, or monoculture in multi‑agent setups.
    
    **Concrete mapping prompts:**
    
    - **Voluntary edges:**
        - “Connections between nodes (operators/agents) are formed when…”
    - **Revocability:**
        - “Any node can unilaterally sever these kinds of links under these conditions…”
    - **Information gradients:**
        - “These types of information may flow freely; these require consent; these are forbidden.”
    - **Anti‑capture rules:**
        - “No single node may gain de facto control if…”

- **Scope statement:**  
    **“This map governs systems that…”**
- **Red‑line clauses:**  
    **“Under no circumstances may the system…”**
- **Operator duties:**  
    **“The operator commits to maintaining…”**
- **System duties:**  
    **“The system commits to maintaining…”**
