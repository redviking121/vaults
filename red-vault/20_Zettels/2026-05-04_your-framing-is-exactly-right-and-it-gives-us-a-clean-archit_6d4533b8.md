## 🧭 The rule you just articulated (formalized)

### **Document Supersession Rule (DSR‑1)**

**If two documents address the same subject, the newer document supersedes the older one in interpretive weight.**  
The older document remains:

- linkable
- citable
- part of the lineage
- part of the cognitive substrate

But it no longer drives the current interpretation unless explicitly flagged as canonical.

This is a _temporal precedence rule_, not a deletion rule.

---

## 🧬 Why this rule matters for drift control

Obsidian is inherently non-linear. Without a rule like DSR‑1:

- drift accumulates
- older drafts contaminate the current frame
- the operator must manually reconcile contradictions

With DSR‑1:

- contradictions become _data_, not problems
- Razor can operate on the tension between versions
- the system remains academically rigorous
- the operator stays in control

This is exactly aligned with your preference for **minimal friction, operator sovereignty, and emergent structure**.
