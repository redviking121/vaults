# **USE ARTICLE — THE VAULT**

_(Canonical Operator Edition)_

## **1. Definition**

The **Vault** is the operator’s structured cognitive substrate — a governed environment where decisions, lineage, and demonstration logic are stabilized, stored, and propagated.  
It is not a diary, not a scrapbook, not a productivity dashboard.  
It is a **substrate engine**.

Two vaults exist by design:

- **Obsidian‑Vault (Product Vault)** — deep substrate, Razor execution, lineage, heavy plugins, 20‑level hierarchy.
- **Ops‑Vault (Admin Vault)** — shallow, stable, minimal, management surface, isolation layer.

The **use article** defines _how_ each vault is used without collapsing them into one.

---

## **2. Purpose & Operator Stance**

The vault exists to:

- Maintain **decision stability**
- Preserve **lineage integrity**
- Provide **substrate continuity**
- Enable **parallel development** without thread‑mesh
- Serve as the **canonical memory** of the system, not the operator

**Operator stance:**

- Treat the vault as a _machine_, not a journal
- Write for the substrate, not for yourself
- Maintain clarity, minimalism, and structural hygiene
- Never let sentiment bleed into structure
- Never collapse Ops and Product layers

## **3. Principles of Use**

### **3.1 Separation of Concerns**

- **Obsidian‑Vault** holds: substrate, Razor logic, governance layers, naming, workflow, demonstration logic, deep notes, lineage.
- **Ops‑Vault** holds: admin procedures, checklists, governance specs, release notes, operational decisions, meta‑architecture.

Nothing crosses the membrane unless explicitly stabilized.

### **3.2 Decision Lifecycle Enforcement**

Every decision follows:

1. **Declared** — captured in Ops‑Vault
2. **Stabilized** — resolved via Razor in Obsidian‑Vault
3. **Propagated** — reflected back into Ops‑Vault as operational truth

This prevents drift, recursion loops, and substrate collapse.

### **3.3 Minimalism as Discipline**

- No decorative notes
- No duplicated structures
- No plugin sprawl
- No narrative inflation
- No “just in case” folders

The vault grows only when the substrate demands it.
