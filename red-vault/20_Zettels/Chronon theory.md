The concept of a **chronon**—a discrete quantum of time—has evolved through several iterations. The relationship between early concepts (notably in the 1970s and earlier) and the "Chronon Field Theory" popularized in the early 1980s (specifically 1982–1984) is a shift from treating time as a **discretized interval** (a "time atom") to treating it as a **dynamic, mediating, force-generating field**. [](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WWUr6wATLDk&t=2)



Here is the relationship between the earlier and later concepts:

1. Early Roots (1970s and Prior)

- **Concept:** Time is not continuous, but rather composed of tiny, indivisible, non-decomposable, discrete segments.
- **Mechanism:** It was often introduced into non-relativistic quantum mechanics to solve problems involving charged particles.
- **Piero Caldirola's Work:** While the idea goes back to the 1920s, Italian physicist Piero Caldirola provided formal mathematical definitions for the chronon in the late 1970s and 1980, defining it based on the mass and charge of the electron.
- **Focus:** The focus was on "discretized quantum mechanics" and treating time as a quantized step rather than an active, fundamental force. [](https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/9706059)
    


2. Sam Vaknin's "Chronon Field Theory" (1982–1984)

- **Relationship:** Vaknin's work builds upon the idea of discrete time but shifts it to a _field theory_.
- **Key Shift:** In Vaknin’s theory (developed in his 1984 dissertation), the chronon is not just a discrete unit (a "time atom"), but an **elementary particle** or "time quark" that _mediates time itself_.
- **Function:** Chronons generate time through their interactions, similar to how particles exchange force in the Standard Model.
- **Time Arrow:** Vaknin postulated different types of time quarks (up, down, color) to explain time asymmetry (the arrow of time) through the interaction of these chronons in a field. [](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WWUr6wATLDk&t=2)
    
    

Summary of Differences

| Feature            | Early Chronon (1970s/Caldirola)   | Chronon Field Theory (Vaknin 1984)   |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| **Primary Nature** | Discretized Interval (time steps) | Dynamic Field of Potentials          |
| **Role of Time**   | A measurement of duration         | A "5th force" mediating interactions |
| **Components**     | "Time atoms"                      | "Time quarks" (particles)            |
| **Mechanism**      | Cut-off in quantum mechanics      | Interaction/Exchange of chronons     |
| **Focus**          | Solving electron movement         | Deriving time asymmetry/gravity      |

In essence, the 1984 theory, as developed by Vaknin, takes the fundamental idea of quantized time ("1970s idea") and turns it into a comprehensive "field theory of time" where events are perturbations in a field, moving from a static "ruler of time" to a dynamic "force of time". [](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GoEne0FW8f0)


_Note: Later developments, particularly by Eytan H. Suchard, have further updated this work to include geometric interpretations and applications to electrogravity._ [](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WWUr6wATLDk&t=2)
