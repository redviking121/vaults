The Shenzhou-20 mission is a significant milestone in China's space exploration program. Launched on April 24, 2025, aboard a Long March 2F rocket from the Jiuquan Satellite Launch Center, the spacecraft is transporting three astronauts—Chen Dong, Chen Zhongrui, and Wang Jie—to the Tiangong space station [youtube.com](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fdWrOQOkgPc) [Yahoo](https://www.yahoo.com/news/china-reveals-shenzhou-20-astronaut-154955919.html). Chen Dong, the mission commander, is a veteran astronaut embarking on his third spaceflight, while Chen Zhongrui and Wang Jie are making their debut in space [Yahoo](https://www.yahoo.com/news/china-reveals-shenzhou-20-astronaut-154955919.html) [Yahoo](https://www.yahoo.com/news/china-launches-shenzhou-20-mission-093110016.html).

The mission involves a six-month stay at the Tiangong space station, where the crew will conduct various tasks, including spacewalks, scientific experiments, and equipment maintenance [Yahoo](https://www.yahoo.com/news/china-reveals-shenzhou-20-astronaut-154955919.html) [Yahoo](https://www.yahoo.com/news/china-launches-shenzhou-20-mission-093110016.html). They will also collaborate with the Shenzhou-19 crew before taking over operations at the station [Yahoo](https://www.yahoo.com/news/china-launches-shenzhou-20-mission-093110016.html) [en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shenzhou_20). This marks the ninth crewed mission to Tiangong and the 20th overall in the Shenzhou program [en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shenzhou_20).
