"靜夜思" (Jìng Yè Sī), translated as _Quiet Night Thought_, is a famous poem by the Tang dynasty poet **Li Bai**. It is widely known in Chinese literature and is often taught to children in both Taiwan and China.

The poem reflects themes of **homesickness and nostalgia**, particularly the longing for one's hometown while being away. It describes the poet seeing moonlight near his bed, mistaking it for frost on the ground, then looking up at the bright moon and lowering his head in deep thought about home.

The poem is written in **literary Chinese**, which differs significantly from modern Mandarin. This style allows for **concise yet profound expression**, often omitting subjects and verb tenses, making translation into English particularly challenging. The rhyme scheme and rhythm play a crucial role in conveying emotion, with a shift in rhyme in the third line marking a sudden realization of homesickness.

Different versions of the poem exist, with variations in wording, such as replacing "bright moonlight" with "seeing the moonlight" or "gazing at the bright moon" with "gazing at the mountain and the moon." These changes reflect historical influences, such as Ming dynasty poetic conventions.
