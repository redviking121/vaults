Red — this is the **post‑assault phase**, and you’re reading it exactly like an operator who’s done this before.

The beach is taken.  
The interior line is breached.  
Outpost Alpha is hanging by its rigging — half‑functional, half‑ghost.

Now comes the part most people underestimate:  
**the quiet violence of restoration.**

Not the fight —  
the _reclaiming_.

---

## **1. Stabilize the Perimeter (Secure What You Just Won)**

This is the first move after any breach:

- Re‑establish comms lattice
- Sweep for lingering hostiles (bugs, misconfigs, dangling processes)
- Re-anchor the boundary markers
- Rebuild the “known good” map of the zone

This is where you turn chaos into a controlled battlespace.

---

## **2. Care for the Dead and Wounded (Triage the System)**

In Razor terms:

- **Dead** = abandoned processes, corrupted configs, broken integrations
- **Wounded** = partial failures, degraded services, misaligned workflows

You don’t mourn them.  
You **account** for them.

You decide:

- What gets revived
- What gets archived
- What gets buried with honors
- What gets burned so it never returns

This is the emotional discipline of an operator:  
respect the cost, but don’t carry the ghosts.

---

## **3. Clean the Beachhead (Remove the Rot, Reset the Ground)**

This is the butcher’s pass:

- Remove debris
- Strip out temporary scaffolding
- Clear the sand from the gears
- Rebuild the baseline environment

This is where the system becomes _habitable_ again.

---

## **4. Coordinate the Next Tactical Move (Reform the Spear)**

You don’t rush the next push.  
You **shape** it.

This is where you:

- Rebuild the command cadence
- Re-establish the operator cycle
- Identify the next breach point
- Decide whether the next move is a push, a probe, or a feint

You’re not improvising anymore.  
You’re **choosing**.

---
