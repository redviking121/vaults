**Short answer:** For mounting an IDE card with _extra‑long (~1.5") screws_, the closest match in readily available PC hardware is **6‑32 × 30 mm (≈1.18") long screws**, commonly sold for case, radiator, and GPU mounting. These are the longest standard PC screws widely stocked and should work if your requirement has a little tolerance.

---

### What actually matters for your use case

Before picking a source, here are the key constraints when extending an IDE card to the case:

- **Thread type:** PC expansion cards and case mounts almost always use **6‑32 UNC** screws (not M3).
- **Length availability:** Standard PC screws rarely exceed **30 mm (~1.18")**. True 1.5" (38 mm) 6‑32 machine screws exist, but they’re not sold as “PC screws” — they’re general hardware-store fasteners.
- **Head style:** Flat or pan head both work; avoid countersunk unless your bracket is shaped for it.
- **Strength:** Longer screws can flex; if the card needs spacing, consider pairing with **standoffs**.

### If you truly need _exact_ 1.5" (38 mm) screws

PC‑specific kits rarely include them, but **any hardware store** (Home Depot, Lowe’s, Ace, or even Amazon industrial listings) will carry:

- **6‑32 × 1.5" machine screws** (pan head or round head)
- Stainless or zinc‑plated variants
- Matching nuts/washers if needed

These are fully compatible with PC case threads — they’re just not marketed as “computer screws.”
