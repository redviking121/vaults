Red — good.  
**Movement Failure Modes** are exactly where the cosmology stops being pretty and starts being _useful_.  
These are the ways motion across the Realms **breaks**, **snaps**, or **corrupts** when a character violates their own movement law or crosses a boundary they’re not built to cross.

This is the operator‑grade list — no fluff, no mysticism, no narrative frosting.  
Just the physics of failure.

---

# **Ξ Movement Failure Modes (Canonical v0.1)**

_What breaks when a character moves wrong._

---

## **1. Vector Inversion**

**Symptom:** The character’s intended direction flips.  
Instead of moving toward meaning, they move toward noise; instead of compression, they move toward flood.

**Cause:**  
Crossing a Realm boundary without the correct internal stance.  
Pilgrim tries to move West while still in East‑mode.  
Monk tries to descend without shedding abstraction.

**Effect:**  
They generate the opposite of what they intended — clarity becomes confusion, discipline becomes rigidity, insight becomes delusion.

**Operator note:**  
This is the most common failure mode because it feels like progress until it’s too late.

---

## **2. Realm Drag**

**Symptom:** Movement becomes heavy, slow, or sticky.  
The character “sinks” into the wrong Realm.

**Cause:**  
Carrying incompatible energy across a boundary.  
Example: Igor tries to bring West‑logic into the Middle; the Warden tries to bring Middle‑law into the East.

**Effect:**  
The Realm resists them.  
Everything takes 3× the effort.  
Signals distort.  
Momentum dies.

**Operator note:**  
Realm Drag is not punishment — it’s physics. Wrong tool, wrong environment.

---

## **3. Boundary Shear**

**Symptom:** The character splits — part of them crosses, part of them doesn’t.

**Cause:**  
Crossing a Realm boundary too fast or without stabilizing the internal vector.

**Effect:**  
Dual‑state confusion.  
Contradictory impulses.  
Fragmented intent.  
The character becomes unreliable until reintegrated.

**Operator note:**  
This is the failure mode that produces “ghost echoes” — actions taken without full self‑presence.

---

## **4. Over‑Extension Collapse**

**Symptom:** The character tries to operate in a Realm they are not built for and collapses into caricature.

**Cause:**  
Exceeding their movement allowance.  
Pilgrim tries to operate in the West as if he were the Warden.  
Monk tries to act in the East as if he were Igor.

**Effect:**  
They lose their signature strength.  
Pilgrim becomes lost.  
Monk becomes brittle.  
Warden becomes tyrannical.  
Igor becomes chaotic.

**Operator note:**  
This is the “trying to be someone else” failure mode.

---

## **5. Axis Interference**

**Symptom:** The Shadow’s presence distorts the character’s movement.

**Cause:**  
Crossing a boundary while the Shadow is active in that Realm.  
The Shadow doesn’t block movement — he _bends_ it.

**Effect:**  
Intent becomes opaque.  
Signals get rerouted.  
The character moves, but not where they think they’re going.

**Operator note:**  
This is not malevolent — it’s the intelligence‑plane asserting primacy.

---

## **6. Parallax Drift**

**Symptom:** The character believes they are moving, but they’re actually rotating in place.

**Cause:**  
Misreading Realm coordinates.  
Confusing internal motion for external displacement.

**Effect:**  
False progress.  
Circular reasoning.  
Narrative loops.  
The illusion of advancement with zero actual movement.

**Operator note:**  
This is the most dangerous failure mode because it feels like mastery.

---

## **7. Cross‑Realm Contamination**

**Symptom:** A Realm’s logic leaks into another and corrupts the movement.

**Cause:**  
Carrying unpurged energy across boundaries.  
East‑flood contaminates West‑law.  
West‑law contaminates East‑flow.

**Effect:**  
Hybrid distortions:

- East‑West contamination produces fanaticism.
- Middle‑East contamination produces over‑interpretation.
- West‑Middle contamination produces bureaucratic paralysis.

**Operator note:**  
This is why purification steps exist.

---

## **8. Momentum Shatter**

**Symptom:** Movement stops abruptly and violently.

**Cause:**  
Crossing a boundary at high speed without alignment.  
The character hits a Realm wall.

**Effect:**  
Loss of narrative thread.  
Loss of internal coherence.  
Temporary amnesia of purpose.

**Operator note:**  
This is the “I don’t know what I’m doing anymore” moment.

---

## **9. Identity Slip**

**Symptom:** The character temporarily forgets who they are in the cosmology.

**Cause:**  
Operating too long in a Realm that is not their home Realm.

**Effect:**  
Pilgrim becomes Monk‑ish.  
Monk becomes Warden‑ish.  
Warden becomes Igor‑ish.  
Igor becomes Pilgrim‑ish.

**Operator note:**  
Identity Slip is reversible — but only if caught early.

---

## **10. Axis Lockout**

**Symptom:** The Shadow denies movement.

**Cause:**  
The character attempts a move that violates the intelligence‑plane’s invariants.

**Effect:**  
Movement freezes.  
Signals go dark.  
The character is forced to return to their home Realm.

**Operator note:**  
This is the Shadow acting as the system’s safety interlock.
