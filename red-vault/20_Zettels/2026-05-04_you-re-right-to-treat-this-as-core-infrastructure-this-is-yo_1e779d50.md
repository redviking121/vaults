You’re right to treat this as core infrastructure—this is your “rapid recovery from crisis” protocol, not a side note.

Let’s be thorough, but keep it operator-grade and non-ceremonial.

---

## Core principles of your new model

- **Non-moral:**  
    **Signal, not sin.** States describe system behavior, not your worth.
    
- **Operator-centric:**  
    You are the authority. The model serves you, not the other way around.
    
- **Reversible and composable:**  
    You can move between states, revisit steps, and layer tools.
    
- **Crisis-optimized:**  
    Designed for _under load_—when cognition is noisy and scaffolding is thin.
    

---

## The state machine for rapid recovery

Think of this as your canonical ladder, refactored.

### 1. Confusion → **Signal Loss**

**Definition:**  
Mapping between reality and your internal model is unreliable.

**Entry signals:**

- “I don’t know what’s going on.”
- Multiple conflicting impulses.
- Freeze, spin, or compulsive context-switching.

**Operator actions:**

- **Stop interpretation:** no meaning-making, no self-judgment.
- **Narrow scope:** identify _one_ concrete, undeniable fact.
- **Anchor:** write a single line:
    
    > “Current state: Confusion. Fact: X.”
    

Exit when: you can state the situation in one clear sentence without spinning.

---

### 2. Danger → **High-Risk State**

**Definition:**  
You’re exposed—internally or externally—and vulnerable to bad moves or old reflexes.

**Entry signals:**

- You feel “on the edge.”
- Old patterns (like ethics-conditions) try to auto-load.
- Stakes feel high, time feels compressed.

**Operator actions:**

- **Reduce exposure:**
    - pause non-essential commitments
    - avoid big irreversible decisions
- **Add scaffolding:**
    - externalize (notes, whiteboard, text)
    - bring in one stabilizing ritual you trust (non-dogmatic)
- **Name the danger:**
    
    > “Danger: [what could realistically go wrong here?]”
    

Exit when: the immediate risk is understood and bounded.

---

### 3. Emergency → **Rapid Reorientation**

**Definition:**  
The acute threat is contained, but the system is still volatile.

**Entry signals:**

- The worst has passed, but you feel shaky.
- You can think, but not deeply.
- You’re tempted to “overcorrect” or make grand resolutions.

**Operator actions:**

- **Re-establish baseline:**
    - sleep, food, hydration, body check
- **Minimal mission check:**
    
    > “What am I actually trying to do in this phase of life/work?”
    
- **Tiny corrective actions:**
    - one small, aligned action that proves you’re back in motion

Exit when: you can hold your mission in mind without panic or collapse.

---

### 4. Normal → **Baseline Operation**

**Definition:**  
System is within expected tolerances.

**Entry signals:**

- Tasks can be done without drama.
- No major threat, no major breakthrough.
- You can plan realistically.

**Operator actions:**

- **Maintain hygiene:**
    - simple routines, light planning, low-ceremony review
- **Observe patterns:**
    - what tends to push you toward Danger or Confusion?

Exit when: either a disturbance pushes you down (Confusion/Danger) or a breakthrough pulls you up (Affluence).

---

### 5. Affluence → **Stable Breakthrough**

**Definition:**  
You’ve achieved a real, repeatable improvement.

**Entry signals:**

- Something is working _better_ than before.
- The improvement feels stable, not a fluke.
- You can describe the change.

**Operator actions:**

- **Capture the win:**
    - what changed, exactly?
    - what made it possible?
- **Protect the gain:**
    - avoid overloading yourself just because things feel good
- **Integrate:**
    - fold the new capability into your “normal” operations

Exit when: the breakthrough is fully integrated or you overextend and slip.

---

### 6. Power → **Self-Sustaining Momentum**

**Definition:**  
High-capacity, resilient, self-directed state.

**Entry signals:**

- You can absorb shocks without losing the plot.
- You feel both capable and grounded.
- You’re not running on mania; it’s stable.

**Operator actions:**

- **Guard against hubris:**
    - no “I’m invincible now” scripts
- **Invest in infrastructure:**
    - build systems that will support you when you’re _not_ in Power
- **Delegate / offload:**
    - move complexity into tools, structures, and habits

Exit when: load increases beyond capacity, or you neglect maintenance and drift downward.

---

## Crisis playbook: the rapid recovery loop

When things go sideways, you can run this as a minimal script:

1. **Identify state:**
    
    - “Am I in Confusion, Danger, or Emergency right now?”
2. **Name it explicitly:**
    
    - “Current state: Danger. Context: [one line].”
3. **Run the state’s minimal actions:**
    
    - Confusion → stop interpretation, anchor one fact.
    - Danger → reduce exposure, add scaffolding, name the risk.
    - Emergency → restore baseline, reconnect to mission, take one small aligned action.
4. **Re-evaluate state after actions.**
    
    - If still in the same state, repeat with smaller scope.
    - If improved, move up the ladder.
5. **Only after stabilization:**
    
    - do any postmortem or meaning-making.

## Disentangling from the old “ethics conditions”

To keep this clean:

- **No confession steps.**  
    You can review behavior, but not as sin—only as signal.
    
- **No external authority.**  
    No imagined “ethics officer,” no invisible judge.
    
- **No fixed “must do X, Y, Z” rituals.**  
    Only _affordances_—tools you choose.
    
- **No permanent labels.**  
    You are never “a Danger person” or “in Power as identity.”  
    It’s just **current system state**.

- or define **very small, concrete actions** you personally like for each state (your own library of moves).
