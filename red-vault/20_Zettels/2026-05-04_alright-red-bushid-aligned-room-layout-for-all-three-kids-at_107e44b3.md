# Bushidō Room Layout

### (One room, three anchor zones, zero interference)

Think of the room as a **dojo floor**:  
every zone has a purpose, a boundary, and a virtue.

---

## **Zone 1 — Rectitude Zone (Kid A: Procedural)**

**Bushidō virtue:** Gi — right action, clean lines  
**Location:** one wall, ideally the one with the least visual noise

### Physical design

- A **single table** with a **clear rectangular surface**
- One **orientation line** (edge of table or taped line)
- No clutter, no competing objects
- Chair aligned square to the table — not angled

### Why it works

Kid A’s nervous system reads this as:  
**“Here is the correct path. Act cleanly.”**

### Operator note

This zone must be the **quietest** and **least dynamic**.  
Motion from other kids should not cross their field of view.

---

## **Zone 2 — Benevolence Zone (Kid B: Pattern‑Completion)**

**Bushidō virtue:** Jin — supportive structure  
**Location:** adjacent wall, but not directly facing Kid A’s line

### Physical design

- A **soft patterned rug** or **fabric panel**
- A shelf with **evenly spaced** items
- Gentle symmetry — nothing high‑frequency
- A small rhythmic element (fan, metronome‑level hum)

### Why it works

Kid B’s system reads:  
**“The world is repeating calmly. You can complete the pattern.”**

### Operator note

This zone must avoid **sharp edges** or **chaotic patterns**.  
It should feel like a steady heartbeat, not a puzzle.

---

## **Zone 3 — Self‑Control Zone (Kid C: Externalizer)**

**Bushidō virtue:** Jisei — disciplined presence  
**Location:** near a **threshold** (doorway, arch, or clear vertical)

### Physical design

- A **single grounding object** (chair, plant, lamp)
- A visible **boundary** (doorframe, corner)
- A place where _you_ can stand or sit with calm posture
- No rapid‑motion objects in this zone

### Why it works

Kid C’s system reads:  
**“Here is the stable presence. Orient yourself.”**

### Operator note

Your posture is part of the architecture here.  
You are the “still sword” — calm, contained, predictable.

---

# How to Prevent Cross‑Contamination

This is the part most people miss.  
Bushidō ethic demands **respect for each warrior’s space**.

### 1. **Lines must not cross**

Kid A’s straight lines should not intersect Kid B’s patterns or Kid C’s motion.

### 2. **Patterns must not dominate**

Kid B’s zone should never be visually loud enough to pull Kid A or C off their anchor.

### 3. **Presence must not overshadow**

Kid C’s relational anchor (you) should not be visible from Kid A’s zone during tasks requiring focus.

### 4. **Zones must be legible at a glance**

Each kid should be able to walk into the room and instantly know:  
**“That’s my place.”**

This is Bushidō’s spatial clarity.
