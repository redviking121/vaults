You can’t directly “sort” our past conversations inside this chat window, but you _can_ navigate or organize them depending on which version of Copilot you’re using. Here’s the landscape so you can get to what you want without hunting around.

---

### 🗂️ How conversation history works today

Copilot keeps **separate chats**, not one giant thread. Each chat is its own session, and you can reopen them from the sidebar (on web) or the conversation list (in the app). What you _can_ do:

- **Reopen previous chats** from the left sidebar (web) or the “Chats” tab (mobile).
- **Rename chats** so they’re easier to find later.
- **Pin important chats** (web) to keep them at the top.
- **Search your chats** (web + mobile) using keywords you remember from the conversation.

What you _can’t_ do yet:

- Automatically sort or group conversations by topic.
- Merge multiple chats into one.
- Filter by date, tag, or category.

---

### 🔍 Practical ways to “sort” your past conversations

These are the methods people use to create order even without built‑in sorting:

- **Use the search bar**  
    Type any keyword you remember (“Xi Razor”, “bibliography”, “operator invariants”). It will surface all chats containing that term.
    
- **Rename chats with a prefix system**  
    For example:
    
    - `Xi / Paradox Razor – lineage`
    - `Xi / Dissemination – diagrams`
    - `Xi / Architecture – operator invariants`
- **Pin your active projects**  
    Keeps your current Xi work at the top.
    
- **Start a “master thread”**  
    Some users create one long-running chat for a project and keep everything there so it’s inherently sorted.
