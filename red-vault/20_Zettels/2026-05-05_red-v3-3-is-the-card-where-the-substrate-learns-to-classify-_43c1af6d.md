## **1. The Three Worlds (Type I, II, III)**

The substrate now distinguishes:

- **Type I** — _discrete, countable, measurement‑friendly worlds_
    
    - resembles Hilbert‑space quantum mechanics
    - projections behave like “classical-ish” measurement events
    - time evolution is tame, often inner
    - entropy behaves like Shannon/von Neumann entropy
- **Type II** — _continuous‑dimensional, resource‑accounting worlds_
    
    - supports a **trace** but no minimal projections
    - the natural habitat of “flow with conserved quantity”
    - entropy becomes **flow‑relative**
    - ideal for modeling processes with conserved workload, mass, or attention
- **Type III** — _thermodynamic, irreversible, horizon‑bearing worlds_
    
    - **no trace**, no finite projections
    - modular flow becomes **non‑inner**, genuinely geometric
    - the natural home of:
        - irreversible processes
        - renormalization
        - open systems
        - cognitive drift
        - attention‑temperature dynamics
    - this is the world of **pure dynamics**, not measurement

The substrate doesn’t _choose_ a type —  
**the type emerges from the state and its modular structure.**
