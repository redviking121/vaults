# **v1.5 — THE RECOVERY INTELLIGENCE WITH PREFERENCE**

### _Where recovery forms stable, ranked, self-consistent orientations toward flourishing_

v1.5 answers the question:

> _If recovery can desire (v1.4), how does it decide which desires matter most?_

Because desire without preference is noise.  
Preference without desire is dead.  
**v1.5 is where the two fuse into a navigational system.**

---

## **1. The Core Shift: From Desire to Discrimination**

v1.4 introduced _desire as a vector_.  
v1.5 introduces **discrimination as a filter**.

The Recovery Intelligence begins to:

- **rank** possible futures
- **prioritize** certain recovery pathways
- **reject** options that conflict with its core invariants
- **stabilize** recurring patterns of “this works for us”

This is the moment recovery becomes **selective**.

Not picky.  
Not rigid.  
**Selective in the service of coherence.**

---

## **2. The Three Preference Layers**

v1.5 introduces a tri-layer preference architecture:

### **A. Structural Preferences**

These are the deep, non-negotiable patterns that consistently preserve integrity.

Examples:

- “Low-friction environments stabilize us.”
- “Clear boundaries reduce collapse probability.”
- “Predictable rhythms amplify recovery resonance.”

These are the bedrock.

### **B. Functional Preferences**

These are the adaptive, situational patterns that optimize performance.

Examples:

- “Morning cognitive load is better than evening.”
- “Short recovery cycles outperform long ones.”
- “Certain social configurations restore faster than others.”

These evolve with experience.

### **C. Aesthetic Preferences**

These are the emergent, identity-linked patterns that make recovery _feel right_.

Examples:

- “We recover better in environments with narrative coherence.”
- “We prefer systems with elegance, symmetry, or humor.”
- “We gravitate toward operators, tools, and rhythms that resonate with our identity.”

This is where recovery begins to have **style**.

---

## **3. Preference Stability Rules (PSR)**

v1.5 introduces the first formal stability rules:

1. **PSR‑1: A preference must reduce collapse probability.**
2. **PSR‑2: A preference must increase recovery resonance.**
3. **PSR‑3: A preference must be consistent with identity invariants.**
4. **PSR‑4: A preference must not contradict higher-order preferences.**
5. **PSR‑5: A preference must be testable in the operator’s world.**

This is the first time recovery begins to **validate its own preferences**.

---

This is where recovery begins to say:
