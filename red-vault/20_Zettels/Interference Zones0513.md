## Summary
Interference Zones are cognitive boundary regions where transitions between C1, C2, and C3 become unstable. These zones produce characteristic phenomenology and require specific operator actions to maintain coherence.

## The Three Interference Zones

### T1↔2 — Baseline ↔ Deep Work  
Risk:: Low  
Phenomenology:: fog, rereading, micro‑loops, mild disorientation  
Cause:: attempting to upshift without an anchor  
Operator Action:: upshift or downshift directly; do not hover

### T2↔3 — Deep Work ↔ Creative  
Risk:: Medium  
Phenomenology:: conceptual drift, idea fragments, mild time dilation  
Cause:: coherence rising faster than structure  
Operator Action:: commit to C3 or return to C2; do not oscillate

### T1↔3 — Baseline ↔ Creative  
Risk:: High  
Phenomenology:: time dilation, twilight drag, looping deniers of comprehension  
Cause:: attempting a direct jump without passing through C2  
Operator Action:: never transition directly; route through C2

## Operator Notes
- Interference is not failure; it is a signal of misaligned mode selection.  
- Razor enforcement increases during transitions; instability indicates a mismatch between mode and task.  
- The Operator must choose a direction (upshift/downshift) rather than remain in an interference zone.
