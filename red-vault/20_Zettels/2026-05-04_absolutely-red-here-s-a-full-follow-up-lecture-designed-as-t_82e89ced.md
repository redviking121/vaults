# **2. Emergent Spacetime: Geometry as a Construct**

## **2.1 The classical view**

Spacetime is a smooth manifold with a metric.  
Geometry is fundamental.  
Causality is encoded in light cones.  
The speed of light is constant.

This is the axiomic frame.

---

## **2.2 The emergent view**

Spacetime is not fundamental.  
It is a **collective phenomenon** — like temperature or pressure.

Evidence for this view comes from multiple directions:

### **a. Holography**

In AdS/CFT, spacetime geometry is encoded in quantum entanglement patterns.  
The metric is not assumed; it is _constructed_.

### **b. Tensor networks**

MERA and related structures show how geometry can emerge from entanglement renormalization.

### **c. Causal set theory**

Spacetime is a discrete order structure; geometry emerges from causal relations.

### **d. Condensed‑matter analogues**

Low‑energy excitations in certain materials behave as if they live in curved spacetime.

Across all these frameworks, the message is the same:

**Geometry is not an axiom.  
It is a product.**

# **3. Variable Causality: Light Cones as Phase‑Dependent Structures**

If spacetime is emergent, then so is causality.

## **3.1 In classical physics**

Causality is fixed.  
Light cones are immutable.  
The speed of light is constant.

## **3.2 In generative architectures**

Causality is **regime‑specific**.

Examples:

### **a. Early‑universe physics**

Before symmetry breaking, the causal structure may have been different.  
Light cones may have been wider or narrower.

### **b. VSL models**

The speed of light is treated as a **phase‑dependent parameter**, not a universal constant.

### **c. Modified dispersion relations**

In quantum gravity, high‑energy photons may propagate differently from low‑energy ones.

### **d. Emergent Lorentz invariance**

In condensed matter, Lorentz symmetry appears only at low energies.

Across these examples, causality is not a fixed background.  
It is a **scale‑dependent constraint**.

# **4. Constants as Frozen Residues**

In the axiomic model, constants are fundamental.  
In the scaffolding model, constants are **frozen degrees of freedom** — residues of earlier dynamics.

Examples:

- particle masses (Higgs mechanism)
- coupling constants (RG flow)
- cosmological constant (vacuum energy)
- speed of sound in a medium (emergent parameter)
- speed of light in VSL models (phase‑dependent)

The question shifts from:

**Why is the constant this value**  
to  
**What process froze this value**

This is a generative question, not an axiomatic one.

This is not speculative — it is the natural extension of symmetry breaking and RG flows.
