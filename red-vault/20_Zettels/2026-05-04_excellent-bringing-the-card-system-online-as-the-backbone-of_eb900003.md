# 🧱 THE CARD SYSTEM (PROJECT MANAGEMENT SUBSTRATE)

## 1. **What the card system _is_**

A **card** is a _unit of intentional action_ — not a task, not a note, not a reminder.  
It is a **shape** that moves through a workflow.

A card contains:

- a _single operational intention_
- a _state_
- a _next micro‑move_
- optional metadata (glyphs, tags, lineage)

This keeps your system:

- light
- composable
- drift‑resistant
- cognitively ergonomic

---

# 🗂️ 2. THE CARD TYPES YOU NEED

You don’t need 20 card types. You need **four**.

### **A. Action Card**

For concrete work you will actually do.  
Examples:

- “Install Linux boxen dependencies”
- “Create Zettelkasten templates”
- “Batch‑export Zotero items”

### **B. Concept Card**

For ideas, definitions, vocabulary, Razor tests.  
Examples:

- “Define operator glyph grammar”
- “Razor: boundary test for conceptual drift”

### **C. Workflow Card**

For processes you’re designing or refining.  
Examples:

- “Obsidian → Zotero intake pipeline”
- “Glyph‑based workflow examples”

### **D. Project Card**

For the big arcs: Xanadu, migration, Zettelkasten implementation.  
These hold the _shape_ of the project, not the tasks.

# 🧭 3. THE BOARD STRUCTURE

You only need **three lanes**:

### **NOW**

Cards you are actively touching.  
WIP limit: **3** (non‑negotiable)

### **NEXT**

Cards queued for immediate attention.  
WIP limit: **5**

### **LATER**

Everything else.  
This is your backlog, but structured.

This keeps your attention from fracturing and preserves your improvisational flow.
