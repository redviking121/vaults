## **One‑page handout: The Xi Micro‑Protocol**

A compact, field‑ready version that fits on a single sheet.

### **Xi: A Practical Method for Working With Patterns**

Xi is a way of seeing and shifting patterns by working directly with awareness, tension, and stance. It requires no beliefs, metaphysics, or prior training.

---

### **1. Establish the Field**

Notice what’s present in awareness—sensations, thoughts, emotions, context.  
Do not interpret or fix anything yet.

### **2. Identify the Stance**

Name the position you’re taking right now (e.g., analyst, protector, skeptic, advocate).  
This reveals how you’re meeting the situation.

### **3. Locate the Tension**

Find the friction, mismatch, or unresolved motion.  
Tension is information.

### **4. Hold the Polarity**

Name the two poles involved (e.g., autonomy vs. belonging, clarity vs. possibility).  
Hold both without collapsing into either.

### **5. Stay in Continuity**

Remain with the tension without escaping, suppressing, or resolving prematurely.  
This is where patterns reorganize.

### **6. Track the Phase‑Shift**

Watch how the pattern moves:  
emergence → tension → crystallization → integration.

### **7. Iterate in Passes**

Return to the pattern with a slightly different stance or frame.  
Each pass increases resolution.

### **8. Make the Frame Explicit**

Articulate the pattern, stance, and motion so others can see it too.  
Shared clarity creates shared reality.

### **9. Apply Deliberate Constraints**

Use a limited vocabulary, diagram, or question to sharpen the pattern.  
Constraints reveal structure.

### **10. Re‑Author**

Once the pattern is clear, rewrite the frame so the tension resolves into a new motion.  
This is agency at the level of meaning.

## **Training Script: How to Teach Xi in 10 Minutes**

This is the version you can use in workshops, salons, or one‑on‑one sessions.

### **Opening (1 minute)**

“We’re going to use Xi to look at a pattern. Xi isn’t about fixing or diagnosing—it’s about seeing the structure of what’s happening so it can reorganize.”

### **Step 1: Establish the Field (1 minute)**

“Pause. Notice what’s present in awareness—thoughts, sensations, emotions, context. Let it all be here.”

### **Step 2: Identify the Stance (1 minute)**

“What position are you taking toward this situation? Analyst? Defender? Critic? Caretaker? Name it.”

### **Step 3: Locate the Tension (1 minute)**

“Where is the friction or mismatch? What feels unresolved or pulled in two directions?”

### **Step 4: Hold the Polarity (2 minutes)**

“What are the two poles? Name them.  
Now hold both at once. Don’t choose. Don’t resolve.”

### **Step 5: Stay in Continuity (1 minute)**

“Stay with the tension. Don’t escape it. Let it show you its structure.”

### **Step 6: Track the Phase‑Shift (1 minute)**

“What’s the motion here? Is something emerging? Tightening? Forming? Integrating?”

### **Step 7: Articulate the Pattern (1 minute)**

“Say what you’re seeing now. Name the motion, not the story.”

### **Step 8: Re‑Author (1 minute)**

“What frame would make this tension generative instead of stuck?  
Rewrite the frame in one sentence.”

### **Closing**

“This is Xi: awareness, stance, tension, polarity, continuity, phase‑shift, articulation, re‑authoring.  
It’s a way of seeing that changes what’s seen.”
