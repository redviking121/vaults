The **Xi Paradox Razor** operates through a deterministic cycle that transforms cognitive tension into structural insight. This mechanism is the engine that prevents the "pointer-loss" events common in high-velocity research [^1].

### Section 3: The Mechanism (Reconcile → Split → Elevate)

The Razor functions as a three-stage recursive loop. When the operator encounters a contradiction—defined as two or more frames, models, or intentions that appear mutually exclusive—the Razor mandates the following sequence [^2].

#### 3.1 The Razor Cycle

1.  **Reconcile:** The operator first tests whether the contradiction is superficial. If the two poles can be integrated into a single, coherent frame without loss of information or structural integrity, the contradiction is resolved through synthesis [^3].
2.  **Split:** If reconciliation fails, the operator tests for *domain divergence*. The contradiction may arise because the poles belong to different cognitive or systemic layers. By splitting the frames—assigning each to its appropriate domain—the operator eliminates the conflict while preserving the utility of both poles [^4].
3.  **Elevate:** If neither reconciliation nor splitting resolves the tension, the contradiction is treated as a *productive paradox*. The operator seeks a higher-order frame—an "elevation"—that encompasses both poles as necessary components of a larger, more complex system. The paradox is not resolved; it is *held* as an engine of insight [^5].

#### 3.2 Failure of Resolution
If a contradiction resists all three stages—Reconcile, Split, and Elevate—the Razor classifies it as **false structure**. In this state, the contradiction is identified as noise or an error in the substrate, and it is discarded to prevent cognitive thrash and pointer evaporation [^6].

#### 3.3 The Operator’s Stance
The mechanism is not merely a logical procedure; it is a *perceptual lock-in*. Once the operator internalizes this cycle, contradictions become persistently legible as structured tensions. The cognitive system can no longer revert to pre-Razor opacity, where contradictions were merely confusing or threatening [^7].
