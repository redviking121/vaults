# Crystalline-Mollusk Field Theory

## Core Premise
The universe is a static manifold (Block Universe). Attention acts as an active, perturbative force that warps the standing waves of this manifold. Local disruptions in these waves manifest as "time" and "materiality."

## Formalism

### 1. The Attention-Warp Formalism
Let the block universe be a static manifold $M$ with a background metric $g_{\mu\nu}$. Attention, $A$, is an active field that perturbs this metric:
$$g'_{\mu\nu} = g_{\mu\nu} + \delta g_{\mu\nu}(A)$$
where $\delta g_{\mu\nu}(A)$ represents the local disruption caused by the active force of attention.

### 2. Time as a Consequence
The experience of "time" ($\tau$) is a consequence of the gradient of this perturbation:
$$\tau \propto \int \nabla A \, dV$$
This implies that time is not a fundamental dimension, but a local phenomenon arising from the interaction between attention and the manifold.

### 3. Materiality as Local Collapse
Materiality is the result of the "collapse" of these standing waves into a localized state due to the intensity of the attention field. When $\delta g_{\mu\nu}(A)$ reaches a critical threshold, the wave function of the manifold undergoes a local phase transition, manifesting as a "material" object or event.

## Implications
- **Local Time:** Time is relative to the local intensity and gradient of the attention field.
- **Materiality:** Matter is a localized, high-intensity disruption in the standing waves of the block universe, sustained by the active force of attention.
- **Human Perception:** Humans perceive a "flow" of time and a "solid" material world because they are embedded within these localized disruptions, taking the consequence (time/matter) for the fundamental reality.
