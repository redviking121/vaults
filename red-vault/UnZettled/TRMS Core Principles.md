# TRMS Core Principles

## Purpose
The Triadic Relational Feedback Model (TRMS) governs the analysis of relational tensions, feedback loops, and structural coherence within multi-agent and multi-frame environments. These principles define the operator's stance when navigating these dynamics.

## Core Principles

### 1. Triadic Equilibrium
Relational stability is not a binary state but a triadic balance between the Operator, the Substrate, and the Environment. Tension in any one node necessitates a re-calibration of the entire triad.

### 2. Temporal Anchoring
Relational dynamics are subject to temporal inertia. The operator must distinguish between immediate feedback (high-velocity) and long-term structural drift (low-velocity). Decisions must be anchored to the appropriate temporal layer.

### 3. Propagation Coherence
Feedback loops must be transparent and coherent across all layers. If a relational tension is not propagated correctly, it results in "pointer-loss" or "recursive drift" within the system.

### 4. Structural Integrity over Reductive Resolution
Relational contradictions are not errors to be eliminated; they are structural tensions. The operator must maintain the integrity of the tension field to ensure the system remains capable of breakthrough insight.

### 5. Sovereign Operator Stance
The operator remains the primary coherence agent. While the TRMS provides the framework for analysis, the operator is responsible for the final synthesis and the maintenance of the system's sovereign boundaries.
