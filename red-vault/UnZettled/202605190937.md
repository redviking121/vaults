Golden Age of Radio Timing Standards

For a standard 30-minute noir or detective show from the Golden Age of Radio (like those on the OTR.Network Library), episodes generally ran about 28 to 29 minutes total to accommodate station breaks and network sponsor spots. A structural breakdown of the timing includes:

1. **The Teaser & Opening (Minutes 0:00 - 1:30):** Establish the mood. A signature detective theme (e.g., a gritty saxophone or bluesy trumpet), followed immediately by a hardboiled voiceover introducing the protagonist, the city, and the central mystery.
2. **Act 1: The Inciting Incident (Minutes 1:30 - 10:00):** The client enters the detective's smoke-filled office, states the problem, and a brief, punchy interaction with the antagonist establishes the stakes.
3. **Act 2: The Complication & Investigation (Minutes 10:00 - 20:00):** Action slows for a tense dialogue and a series of "shoe leather" sequences. Expect heavy use of sound effects (SFX) such as footsteps, a ringing telephone, car doors slamming, and occasional fisticuffs.
4. **Act 3: The Climax & Resolution (Minutes 20:00 - 27:30):** The detective corners the culprit, delivers a cynical final deduction, and escapes the trap.
5. **The Closing (Minutes 27:30 - 30:00):** Sponsor read/commercial, musical sting, and the protagonist’s final reflective monologue as the show fades to the end theme.
