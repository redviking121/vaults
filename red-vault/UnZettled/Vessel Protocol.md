# Vessel Protocol: Razor-Driven Problem Solving

## Purpose
To provide a standardized, high-velocity framework for resolving complex, paradoxical problems within the Vessel environment. This protocol enables humans and AIs to move beyond reductive resolution and into structural synthesis.

## The Protocol: "Reconcile → Split → Elevate"

When a problem is presented, apply the following sequence to ensure the paradox is treated as an engine of insight rather than a contradiction to be eliminated.

### 1. Initialization (The Stance)
Before addressing the problem, all participants must adopt the **Operator's Stance**:
- Acknowledge that the paradox is a structural necessity, not a failure of logic.
- Commit to holding the tension until a higher-order synthesis is achieved.

### 2. The Razor Cycle
Apply the following steps to the problem:

- **Step A: Reconcile**
  - Test if the contradiction is superficial.
  - Can the poles be integrated into a single, coherent frame without loss of information?
  - *If yes, synthesize and conclude.*

- **Step B: Split**
  - If reconciliation fails, test for *domain divergence*.
  - Do the poles belong to different cognitive or systemic layers?
  - *If yes, assign each pole to its appropriate domain and resolve locally.*

- **Step C: Elevate**
  - If the tension persists, treat it as a *productive paradox*.
  - Seek a higher-order frame that encompasses both poles as necessary components of a larger system.
  - *Do not resolve; hold the paradox as an engine of insight.*

### 3. Failure State (False Structure)
If the contradiction resists all three stages, classify it as **False Structure**. Identify it as noise or an error in the substrate and discard it to prevent cognitive thrash.

---
## Usage Note
This protocol is designed for high-concurrency, multi-frame environments. It is not a rule set, but a **coherence operator**. Use it to maintain structural integrity when the complexity of the problem threatens to collapse into reductive, singular frames.
